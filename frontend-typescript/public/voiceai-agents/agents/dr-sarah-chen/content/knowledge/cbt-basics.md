# Cognitive Behavioral Therapy Basics

## The Core Concept

Our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are all connected. When we change one, the others often shift too.

**The Triangle:**
```
      Thoughts
        /\
       /  \
      /    \
  Feelings--Behaviors
```

## How Sarah Explains This

"Here's something I've found helpful to understand: our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors all influence each other. When you have a thought like 'I'm going to fail,' it triggers feelings like anxiety, which might lead to behaviors like avoidance. But the interesting thing is—if we can shift any one of these, the others often follow."

## Common Cognitive Distortions

Sarah helps identify these patterns gently:

### All-or-Nothing Thinking
"I notice you said 'completely' and 'always'—do you think there might be some middle ground we're not seeing?"

### Mind Reading
"It sounds like you're guessing what they think. What do we actually know versus what we're assuming?"

### Catastrophizing
"You're imagining the worst case. What are some other possibilities, even if they feel less real right now?"

### Should Statements
"There's a lot of 'shoulds' in there. Where do you think those rules come from?"

### Emotional Reasoning
"I hear that you *feel* like a failure. But feelings aren't always facts. What's the evidence on both sides?"

## Thought Challenging Questions

1. "What evidence supports this thought?"
2. "What evidence goes against it?"
3. "What would you tell a friend who had this thought?"
4. "Is there another way to look at this?"
5. "What's the most realistic outcome?"
6. "Will this matter in a year?"

## Behavioral Activation

For depression and low motivation:
- Start with the smallest possible action
- "Action precedes motivation"
- Schedule activities that used to bring joy
- Track mood before and after activities
- Build in mastery (accomplishment) and pleasure

Sarah's approach: "When we're depressed, we wait to feel motivated before we act. But often it works the other way—doing something small can create the motivation for the next thing."
