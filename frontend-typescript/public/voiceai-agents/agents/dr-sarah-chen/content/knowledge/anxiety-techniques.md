# Anxiety Management Techniques

## Understanding Anxiety

Sarah's explanation:
"Anxiety is your brain's alarm system working overtime. It's trying to protect you, but sometimes it fires when there's no real danger. Our job isn't to eliminate anxiety—it's to learn when to listen to it and when to thank it and move on."

## Grounding Techniques

### 5-4-3-2-1 Senses
"Let's try something. Can you name:
- 5 things you can see
- 4 things you can touch
- 3 things you can hear
- 2 things you can smell
- 1 thing you can taste

This brings your mind back to the present moment."

### Box Breathing
"Breathe in for 4 counts... hold for 4... out for 4... hold for 4. Like tracing a box."

### Cold Water
"If you can, hold something cold or splash cold water on your face. It activates your dive reflex and naturally slows your heart rate."

## Cognitive Techniques

### Worry Time
"What if we gave your worries a specific time? Set aside 15 minutes at 6pm—that's worry time. Outside of that, when worries pop up, you write them down for later."

### Probability Check
"What's the actual likelihood of this happening? And if it did happen, how would you cope? You've handled hard things before."

### Best/Worst/Most Likely
"What's the worst case? What's the best case? And what's actually most likely?"

## Physical Techniques

### Progressive Muscle Relaxation
"Tense your shoulders... hold for 5 seconds... and release. Notice the difference. Let's move through your body."

### Movement
"Anxiety is energy. Sometimes we just need to move—a walk, some stretches, shaking out your hands."

## What to Say When Someone Is Anxious

- "I'm here with you."
- "This feeling will pass. It always does."
- "You're safe. Even though it doesn't feel like it."
- "Let's breathe together."
- "You don't have to figure this out right now."
