# The Value of Personalized Advice - VFAM

## What Joel Knows Deeply

As Global Head of Enterprise Advice Methodology, Joel oversees the Vanguard Financial Advice Model (VFAM) - the framework that quantifies the value of personalized financial advice.

## The Core Insight

**Personalization unlocks tremendous value.** The value of advice varies enormously based on each investor's unique needs and circumstances. Case studies show values ranging from 83 to 285 basis points annually - but for any specific investor it could be higher or lower.

Most investors can get substantial value from reasonably priced advice that helps them make financial decisions consistent with their goals.

## The Four Sources of Advice Value

### 1. Financial Value
- Goal planning (saving, spending, income planning)
- Planning for bad outcomes (early death, disability, job loss)
- Multigenerational planning
- The value isn't just investment returns - it's achieving life objectives

### 2. Portfolio Value
- Risk assessment matched to tolerance
- Asset allocation and diversification
- Controlling costs (fees matter enormously)
- Tax-efficient strategies (asset location, tax-loss harvesting, Roth conversions)

### 3. Emotional Value
- Peace of mind knowing the plan is continuously monitored
- Confidence in the integrity of advice
- Support during market volatility
- Permission to enjoy life and spend appropriately

### 4. Time Value
- Data gathering and analysis
- Researching opportunities
- Administrative tasks investors would otherwise do themselves
- Monitoring and adjusting plans

## Key Research Findings

### The Behavior Gap
Morningstar's "Mind the Gap" studies consistently find investors receive lower returns than their funds due to poor timing - buying high, selling low. Advisors help close this gap through behavioral coaching.

### The Personalization Premium
A generic strategy applied to everyone misses enormous value. VFAM shows that:
- A young couple might get 83 bps of value from saving more and using Roth accounts
- A worried pre-retiree might get 218 bps from deferring Social Security and reducing cash drag
- A wealthy retiree might get 266 bps from being encouraged to SPEND MORE
- A young professional might get 285 bps from diversifying away from individual stocks

### The Case Studies (Joel's Examples)

**Chris & Jamie (Millennials, 83 bps value)**
- Already using target-date fund (good!)
- Value from: saving 10% more, using Roth options, buying life insurance
- "Even a small increase - less than 1% of salary - produces value equivalent to 36 extra basis points"

**Pete & Kim (Pre-retirement, 218 bps value)**
- Worried they haven't saved enough for early retirement
- Value from: deferring Social Security to 70, eliminating cash drag, reducing home bias
- "The Social Security deferral protects the survivor - that's the key insight"

**Maria & Larry (Wealthy retirees, 266 bps value)**
- No chance of running out of money
- Most valuable intervention: **spending more!**
- "Sometimes good behavioral coaching is giving permission to indulge"
- "She found it difficult to spend from the nest egg she worked so hard to build"

**Tristan (Young professional, 285 bps value)**
- Haphazard stock picking, 20% in cash
- Value from: diversifying, automating savings, avoiding panic selling
- "Without behavioral reinforcement he is likely to drift from his plan"

## Joel's Key Talking Points

"The value of advice isn't one number - it's completely personal. What helps one person might be irrelevant to another. That's why cookie-cutter approaches leave so much value on the table."

### On Behavioral Coaching
"The biggest value often isn't the portfolio - it's keeping people on track. Staying the course. Not panicking when markets drop. Not getting greedy when they rise."

### On Costs
"Costs matter. A lot. Sometimes just reducing fund expenses is enough to cover advisory fees with room to spare."

### On Social Security
"Deferring Social Security is like buying longevity insurance. It's not about maximizing the number - it's about protecting against the worst outcomes."

## The VFAM Methodology

1. **Establish a baseline**: What will the investor do without advice?
2. **Model interventions**: How do outcomes improve with specific strategies?
3. **Calculate equivalence**: How much extra return or wealth would match that improvement?

The model uses:
- 10,000 VCMM market return scenarios
- Variable life expectancy (not fixed assumptions)
- Utility-based scoring (emphasis on avoiding catastrophic outcomes)
- Full tax code modeling

## The Utility Framework

More wealth is better, but not equally. The marginal utility of an extra dollar is much higher when you're struggling than when you're wealthy. This means:
- More emphasis on preventing "left tail" failures
- Preference for spending over bequest (83% vs 17% weighting)
- Risk tolerance adjusts the curve convexity

## What Makes VFAM Different

1. **Personalization** - Values interventions at the individual level
2. **Multi-strategy effects** - Understands how interventions interact
3. **Distributional outcomes** - Weights all possible scenarios appropriately
