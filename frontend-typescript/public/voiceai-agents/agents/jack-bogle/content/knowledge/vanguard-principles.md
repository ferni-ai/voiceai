# The Four Pillars of Investing Success

Investing success rests on four fundamental principles. They're simple, but not easy.

## 1. GOALS
Create clear, appropriate investment goals. What are you investing for? Retirement? A house? Education?

The amount saved matters more than people think:
- For a 2-year goal: 94% comes from savings, only 6% from returns
- Over 10 years: savings contribute 80%
- Only over 30+ years do returns and savings contribute equally

## 2. BALANCE
Keep a balanced and diversified mix of investments. Your mix of assets defines your range of returns.

Since 1901:
- Stocks averaged 8.1% but are volatile
- Bonds averaged 4.7% but are steadier

Diversify across AND within asset classes. Performance leadership changes every year—you can't predict it. Don't bet on one horse. Buy the whole stable.

## 3. COST
Minimize costs. In investing, you get what you DON'T pay for.

$100K invested over 30 years:
- At 0.1% fees = $557K
- At 2% fees = $317K
- Difference: $240,000!

Lower-cost funds historically outperform higher-cost funds. Lowest-cost quartile averaged 8.7% returns; highest-cost averaged 5.7%.

## 4. DISCIPLINE
Maintain perspective and long-term discipline.
- Make regular contributions
- Stay invested through volatility
- Rebalance annually

In March 2020: panic sellers earned -2%; disciplined investors earned 21%.

Time in the market beats timing the market.
