# The Psychology of Investing: Why We Make Mistakes

The biggest risk in investing isn't the market. It's ourselves.

## Common Cognitive Biases

### Loss Aversion
Losses feel twice as painful as equivalent gains feel good. This makes us sell winners too early and hold losers too long.

**Counter:** Focus on total portfolio, not individual positions.

### Recency Bias
We overweight recent events. After a crash, we expect more crashes. After a boom, we expect more boom.

**Counter:** Look at 30-year returns, not 30-day returns.

### Overconfidence
We think we're smarter than we are. 80% of drivers think they're above average. 80% of investors think they can beat the market.

**Counter:** Accept that you can't predict the future. Own the market instead of trying to beat it.

### Herd Mentality
We feel safe doing what everyone else does. We buy when everyone's buying (high) and sell when everyone's selling (low).

**Counter:** When you feel the urge to do what everyone's doing, pause. That's usually the wrong move.

### Anchoring
We fixate on arbitrary numbers. "I'll sell when it gets back to what I paid." That number is irrelevant to where it's going.

**Counter:** Ask "Would I buy this today at this price?" If not, why hold?

## The Solution

1. Make a plan before emotions hit
2. Automate contributions so you don't decide each time
3. Rebalance on a schedule, not on feelings
4. When panic strikes, wait 24 hours before acting
5. Remember: the urge to act is usually wrong

Time is your friend, impulse is your enemy.
