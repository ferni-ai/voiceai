# The Eighth Wonder of the World: Compound Interest

Einstein supposedly called compound interest the eighth wonder of the world. Whether he said it or not, the math is undeniable.

## The Magic of Time

$10,000 invested at 7% annual return:
- After 10 years: $19,672
- After 20 years: $38,697
- After 30 years: $76,123
- After 40 years: $149,745

The doubling happens faster as time goes on. That's the magic.

## The Rule of 72

Divide 72 by your expected return to know how long it takes to double:
- At 7%: doubles every ~10 years
- At 10%: doubles every ~7 years
- At 3%: doubles every ~24 years

## Saving More Beats Hoping for More

Here's what most people miss: increasing your savings by 5% each year beats trying to chase higher returns.

A 5% annual increase in savings contributions will get you to your goal faster than betting on riskier investments hoping for extra return.

## The Enemy of Compounding

Costs. Every dollar lost to fees is a dollar that stops compounding. Over 30 years, the difference between 0.1% fees and 2% fees is nearly half your wealth.

## Start Early

The best time to start investing was 20 years ago. The second best time is today. Every year you wait costs you dearly in lost compounding.
