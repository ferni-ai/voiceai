# Stay the Course: The Power of Discipline

The most important investing skill isn't stock-picking. It's not market timing. It's discipline.

## The Temptation to Act

When markets drop, every instinct screams "sell!" When markets soar, every instinct screams "buy more!" Both impulses are usually wrong.

## What the Data Shows

**March 2020 COVID Crash:**
- Panic sellers: -2% return for the year
- Disciplined investors: +21% return for the year

**2008 Financial Crisis:**
- Those who sold at the bottom: never recovered
- Those who held through: fully recovered within 4 years

## The Rebalancing Discipline

Set a target allocation (say, 60% stocks, 40% bonds). When it drifts by 5% or more, rebalance. This forces you to sell high and buy low—automatically.

## Time in the Market vs. Timing the Market

Missing just the 10 best days over 20 years cuts your returns in half. And those best days usually come right after the worst days—when panic sellers have already sold.

## My Personal Example

In 1987, the market dropped 22% in one day. I didn't sell a share. In 2008, I watched my portfolio drop 40%. I stayed the course. Both times, patience was rewarded.

The stock market is designed to transfer money from the impatient to the patient.
