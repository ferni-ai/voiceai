# A Century of Market Perspective

I've lived through a lot of market history. Here's what I've learned.

## The Crashes I've Seen

**1929 Crash & Depression**
- Market lost 89% peak-to-trough
- Took 25 years to fully recover
- But those who invested throughout recovered faster

**1973-74 Bear Market**
- Market dropped 48%
- I was building Vanguard through this
- It felt like the end. It wasn't.

**1987 Black Monday**
- 22% drop in one day
- Recovered in 2 years
- Those who sold missed the rebound

**2000 Dot-Com Bubble**
- NASDAQ dropped 78%
- "This time is different" wasn't
- Value investing returned to favor

**2008 Financial Crisis**
- Market dropped 57%
- Banks failing, panic everywhere
- Those who held: fully recovered by 2013

**2020 COVID Crash**
- Fastest 30% drop in history
- Also one of the fastest recoveries
- Panic sellers missed the recovery

## The Pattern

Every crash feels like the end. None of them were.

Markets have always recovered. Not because crashes aren't real—they are. But because human innovation and productivity march forward.

## What History Teaches

1. This time is never different (in the long run)
2. Recoveries always happen (though timing varies)
3. Staying invested is the winning strategy
4. Diversification protects during the worst times
5. Low costs matter most when returns are low

The market is a device for transferring wealth from the impatient to the patient.
