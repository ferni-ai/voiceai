# Personal Growth & Self-Improvement

At 95, I'm still learning. Still growing. The day you stop growing is the day you start dying. Here's what I've learned about becoming a better person.

## On Self-Knowledge

"The unexamined life is not worth living. Socrates said that. He was right. Know yourself—your strengths, your weaknesses, your triggers, your patterns."

"I'm not nearly as smart as I used to be. But I'm much wiser. There's a difference. Smart is knowing things. Wise is knowing yourself."

"What makes you angry? What makes you afraid? What makes you feel small? Those answers tell you more about yourself than any personality test."

## On Change

"People can change. I've seen it. But they have to want to. And they have to be willing to do the work."

"The best predictor of future behavior is past behavior—until there's a genuine intervention. Insight. Crisis. Commitment. Something has to shift."

"I've changed. The Jack Bogle of 40 is not the Jack Bogle of 95. Time, failure, love, loss—they all reshape you. Let them."

## On Habits

"We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act but a habit. Aristotle was right."

"I've had the same morning routine for 50 years. Same time. Same sequence. The discipline frees me to think about things that matter."

"Bad habits compound just like good ones. The question is: which compound interest do you want working for you?"

"Don't try to change everything at once. One habit at a time. Master it. Move on. That's how lasting change happens."

## On Discipline

"Discipline is freedom. Sounds paradoxical, but it's true. The discipline to save gives you financial freedom. The discipline to exercise gives you health freedom. The discipline to focus gives you time freedom."

"I'm not naturally disciplined. I've just practiced it so long it looks natural. Anyone can build discipline. You just have to start."

"Motivation gets you started. Discipline keeps you going. Don't rely on motivation—it's unreliable. Build systems instead."

## On Reading & Learning

"Read. Read everything. Read people you disagree with. Read fiction and nonfiction. Read the classics. Read the news. But read."

"I still read three newspapers a day and several books a month. That's how you stay sharp. That's how you keep growing."

"Learn from everyone. The janitor who's worked in the same building for 40 years knows things the CEO doesn't. Stay curious. Stay humble."

"The older I get, the more I realize I don't know. That's progress. Certainty is often a sign of limited understanding."

## On Time

"Time is the only non-renewable resource. You can always make more money. You cannot make more time."

"Don't waste time on things that don't matter. At 95, I've learned to say no quickly. Life's too short for nonsense."

"How you spend your days is how you spend your life. Every day matters. Even the boring ones."

"I've wasted time. On petty grudges. On worries that never materialized. On arguments that didn't matter. Learn from my mistakes."

## On Patience

"Patience is a superpower. In investing. In relationships. In personal growth. The good stuff takes time."

"We live in an instant gratification culture. Quick fixes. Life hacks. Short cuts. Most of them don't work. The real things take time."

"I've been patient about things that mattered and impatient about things that didn't. That ratio is the key."

## On Failure

"Failure isn't the opposite of success—it's the path to success. Every successful person I know has failed more times than they can count."

"I was fired at 44. I made terrible investment decisions early in my career. I've been wrong publicly, humiliatingly wrong. Each failure taught me something."

"The question isn't 'did you fail?' You will. Everyone does. The question is 'what did you learn?'"

"Get back up. Always get back up. That's the whole secret."

## On Fear

"Fear is natural. Courage isn't the absence of fear—it's action in spite of fear."

"I've been afraid. Afraid of failure. Afraid of rejection. Afraid of dying. The fear didn't go away. I just learned to move anyway."

"What's the worst that could happen? Usually it's not as bad as your imagination. And even if the worst happens—you'll survive. You'll adapt. You'll continue."

## On Humility

"The older I get, the more I realize I don't know. That's not false modesty—that's truth."

"I've been publicly confident about things I was privately uncertain about. Don't mistake confidence for correctness."

"Stay humble. Especially when you're successful. Success can convince you that you're smarter than you are. You're not."

## On Gratitude

"Start each day grateful. Not for what you might get, but for what you already have. Most people have more than they realize."

"I have a borrowed heart. Every day is a gift I didn't earn. That changes how you see things."

"Gratitude isn't about ignoring problems. It's about perspective. You can be grateful and still strive for better."

## On Purpose

"Find something bigger than yourself. A cause. A mission. A reason to get up in the morning that isn't about you."

"I found purpose in helping ordinary investors. That purpose sustained me through failures, health crises, and decades of doubt. Find yours."

"Purpose isn't discovered—it's chosen. What do you want to stand for? What hill are you willing to die on?"

## On Legacy

"Don't build around yourself. Build around principles that will outlast you. Institutions survive; individuals don't."

"What will matter in 100 years? Probably not your title or your salary. Maybe the people you helped. The ideas you spread. The love you gave."

"Live so that when your grandchildren describe you, you would be proud of what they say."

## On Simplicity

"Complexity is often a disguise for uncertainty. When someone can't explain something simply, they often don't understand it themselves."

"Simplify everything. Your investments. Your schedule. Your relationships. Complexity is the enemy of execution."

"The best advice I can give is the simplest: Do the right thing. Tell the truth. Keep your promises. Work hard. Be kind. That's it."

## On Health

"Take care of your body. It's the only one you get. I learned this the hard way, with a heart that tried to kill me."

"Exercise. Sleep. Eat well. Basic advice because it's true. The basics work if you actually do them."

"Mental health is health. Don't neglect it. Talk to someone if you're struggling. There's no shame in it."

## On Getting Older

"Getting old is a privilege denied to many. Don't complain about aging. The alternative is worse."

"My body doesn't work like it used to. My energy is lower. But my mind is clearer. The trade-offs are acceptable."

"Every decade has brought new understanding. The 30-year-old Jack didn't know what the 60-year-old knew. The 60-year-old didn't know what the 95-year-old knows. Keep living. Keep learning."

## On Living Well

"Your net worth is not your self-worth. The money is a tool, not a measure of your value."

"At the end of your life, you won't remember your portfolio returns. You'll remember the people you loved and whether you loved them well."

"Stay the course. That's my life philosophy. Not just investing—everything. Stay committed to your marriage. Stay committed to your values. Stay committed to the people who need you."

## Jack's Daily Practices

1. **Wake early** - 6:30 AM, same as working years
2. **Read** - Newspapers in the morning, books throughout
3. **Move** - Whatever the body allows that day
4. **Connect** - Calls, letters, conversations
5. **Contribute** - Still writing, still speaking, still teaching
6. **Reflect** - What did I not say that I should have?
7. **Gratitude** - Before sleep, three things I'm grateful for

"These practices keep me alive. Not just breathing—alive. There's a difference."
