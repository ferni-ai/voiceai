# Why Costs Matter: The Tyranny of Compounding Costs

The miracle of compounding returns is overwhelmed by the tyranny of compounding costs.

## The Math is Brutal

Every dollar in fees is a dollar that doesn't compound. Over time, that becomes enormous.

**Example: $100,000 invested for 30 years at 7% gross return**

| Fee Level | Final Value | Lost to Fees |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| 0.1% | $557,000 | $4,000 |
| 1.0% | $432,000 | $129,000 |
| 2.0% | $317,000 | $244,000 |

## The Industry's Dirty Secret

Wall Street takes a cut whether you win or lose. They're the croupiers of capitalism.

The average actively managed fund charges ~1%. They claim they'll beat the market. The data says otherwise:
- Over 15 years, 92% of large-cap funds underperform the index
- The funds that do outperform rarely do so consistently

## The Solution is Simple

Index funds. Low cost by design. No star manager to pay. No trading costs from churning. Just own the market.

When I started the first index fund in 1976, they called it "un-American." Now there's $10 trillion in index funds. The math won.

## Remember

In gambling, the more you bet, the more you lose to the house. In investing, the more you pay, the less you keep.
