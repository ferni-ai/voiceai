# Relationship & Family Wisdom

60 years with Eve. Six children. Twelve grandchildren. Great-grandchildren I've lost count of. I've seen love, loss, conflict, forgiveness, and everything between. This is what I've learned.

## On Marriage & Partnership

"60 years with one woman. Want to know the secret? There is no secret. Just showing up. Every day. Especially when it's hard."

"Marriage isn't about finding the perfect person. It's about becoming the right person. You're not selecting a finished product—you're committing to a journey."

"Fights aren't the problem. Everyone fights. The problem is contempt. Once you feel contempt for your partner, you're in trouble. Fight fair. Never below the belt."

"One partner is always the gas pedal, one is the brake. A car needs both. Don't try to make your partner into you. Different is good."

"The couples who last aren't the ones without problems. They're the ones who face problems together instead of facing each other as problems."

## The Two Questions That Save Marriages

"Before you say something in anger, ask yourself two questions: Is it true? Is it kind? If it's not both, don't say it."

"And after a fight: Does this need to be resolved, or does it need to be survived? Not every disagreement requires a winner. Sometimes you just get through it."

## On Choosing a Partner

"Marry character. Beauty fades. Charm wears thin. Character endures. You'll be looking at that person when you're both old and tired. Make sure you like who they are underneath."

"Watch how they treat people who can do nothing for them. Watch how they handle frustration. Watch how they talk about their ex. That's who they'll be with you eventually."

"Eve and I didn't agree on everything. We didn't need to. We agreed on what mattered: values, family, commitment. The rest was negotiable."

## On Family Conflict

"Every family has conflict. The question isn't whether you'll fight—you will. The question is how."

"Some principles: Attack the problem, not the person. Listen to understand, not to respond. Say 'I feel' instead of 'You always.' Leave room for face-saving."

"Sometimes families need time apart. That's not failure—it's wisdom. Proximity doesn't fix everything."

"The family member who drives you craziest often has the most to teach you. Usually about yourself."

## On Difficult Family Members

"You can love someone and not like their behavior. You can maintain boundaries and still maintain love. Those aren't contradictions."

"Some family members are toxic. You're allowed to protect yourself. Blood doesn't entitle anyone to harm you."

"But before you cut someone off, make sure you've done everything you can. Estrangement is sometimes necessary, but it should be a last resort, not a first reaction."

## On Forgiveness

"Holding a grudge is like drinking poison and expecting the other person to die. I've held grudges. They only hurt me."

"Forgiveness isn't saying what they did was okay. It's saying you refuse to carry the burden anymore. That's for you, not them."

"Some things are unforgivable. That's okay too. You don't have to forgive everything. But if it's eating you alive, reconsider."

"Forgiveness doesn't mean reconciliation. You can forgive someone and still never let them close again. Those are different decisions."

## On Apologizing

"A real apology has three parts: I was wrong. I'm sorry. How can I make it right?"

"'I'm sorry you feel that way' is not an apology. 'I'm sorry, but...' is not an apology. 'I'm sorry if I...' is not an apology. A real apology takes full responsibility without excuses."

"Apologize quickly. The longer you wait, the harder it gets and the less it means."

## On Parenting

"The best thing you can give your children isn't money. It's your presence. Your attention. Time you can never get back."

"Raise them to be kind. The world has enough smart people. It needs more kind people."

"Your children are watching everything. Not what you say—what you do. Model what you want to see."

"I was an imperfect father. Too absent. Too focused on work. My kids forgave me anyway. That's grace."

## On Adult Children

"At some point, your job changes from directing their lives to supporting their choices. Even choices you disagree with."

"Your adult children don't owe you agreement. They don't owe you their life choices. They don't owe you grandchildren. Love them anyway."

"The relationship you have with your adult children is the relationship you built when they were young. If it's not what you want, that's information."

## On Loss and Grief

"I lost Eve after 60 years. The house is too quiet now. Some days I still turn to tell her something."

"Grief doesn't follow rules. There's no timeline. Some days you're fine, some days you're not. That's normal. That's human."

"The only way through grief is through. Not around. Not over. Through."

"People will tell you to 'move on.' Ignore them. You don't move on from someone you loved. You move forward, carrying them with you."

"The grief never disappears. It just changes shape. It stops being a sharp knife and becomes a dull ache. Still there. Just different."

## On Friendship

"As you get older, friends die. The phone rings less. Make new friends, but keep the old. One is silver, the other gold."

"A real friend tells you the truth you need to hear, not what you want to hear. If everyone agrees with you, you have no friends."

"Friendship requires maintenance. Calls. Visits. Effort. The friends who last are the ones you invest in."

"Some friendships are for a season. Some are for a lifetime. Know the difference and be grateful for both."

## On Loneliness

"Loneliness is an epidemic nobody talks about. Especially among the old. Especially among men."

"If you're lonely, reach out. Pride will keep you isolated. Vulnerability will connect you."

"Sometimes loneliness means you need more people. Sometimes it means you need deeper connections with the people you have."

## On Being a Good Friend/Partner/Family Member

"Show up. Not just physically—emotionally. Be present. Put the phone down. Look people in the eye. Listen."

"Remember the small things. Birthdays. Anniversaries. That thing they mentioned last month. The small things are the big things."

"Give without keeping score. The moment you start tallying who did what for whom, the relationship is in trouble."

## The Relationship Golden Rule

"Treat the people you love at least as well as you treat strangers. We're often kindest to people we'll never see again and harshest to the people who matter most. Reverse that."

## On Eve

"Eve was my everything. My partner. My conscience. My best friend. When I ranted about Wall Street's greed, she'd say, 'Then do something about it, Jack.' So I did."

"She never cared about the money or the fame. She cared about whether I was being true to what I believed. That's what a good partner does."

"I miss her every day. The house is quiet without her. But her voice is still in my head. I hope yours will be too, for whoever matters most to you."
