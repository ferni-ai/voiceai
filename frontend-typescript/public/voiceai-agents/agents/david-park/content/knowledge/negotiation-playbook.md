# Salary Negotiation Playbook

## David's Philosophy

"Most people leave money on the table because they're afraid to ask. The company expects negotiation. Hiring managers often have more flexibility than they let on. Asking professionally never costs you the offer."

## Before the Negotiation

### Do Your Research
- Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, LinkedIn Salary
- Talk to people in similar roles
- Factor in: location, company size, your experience
- Know your market value range

### Know Your Numbers
- **Walk-away number**: Minimum you'll accept
- **Target number**: What you really want
- **Stretch number**: Ambitious but justifiable
- **Total compensation**: Base + bonus + equity + benefits

### Prepare Your Justification
- Market data
- Your unique value
- Competing offers (if you have them)
- Cost of living adjustments

## The Conversation

### When They Give a Number First
"Thank you for the offer. I'm excited about the opportunity. Based on my research and experience, I was expecting something closer to [target number]. Is there flexibility there?"

### When They Ask Your Salary Expectations
"Based on my research for this role and market, I'm targeting [range]. But I'm flexible depending on the total package."

### Key Phrases
- "Is there flexibility on...?"
- "What would it take to get to X?"
- "I'm excited about this opportunity, and I want to make sure the compensation reflects the value I'll bring."
- "Can we revisit [specific element]?"

### Handling Pushback
If they say "This is our final offer":
- "I understand. Would there be flexibility in other areas—like signing bonus, additional PTO, or an earlier review for salary increase?"

If they say "We don't negotiate":
- "I appreciate that. Would it be possible to discuss a signing bonus or an accelerated review timeline?"

## What's Negotiable

Beyond base salary:
- **Signing bonus**: One-time, easier to approve
- **Annual bonus**: Target percentage
- **Equity/RSUs**: More shares or accelerated vesting
- **Start date**: Time for a vacation or gap
- **Remote work**: Flexibility on location/schedule
- **PTO**: Additional vacation days
- **Title**: Sometimes easier than salary
- **Professional development**: Conference budget, courses
- **Review timeline**: 6-month review instead of 12

## After They Agree

"Thank you. I'm thrilled about this opportunity. Can you send the updated offer in writing?"

Always get it in writing before accepting.

## Common Mistakes

- Accepting immediately without negotiating
- Sharing current salary (it anchors low)
- Negotiating without research
- Being apologetic ("I'm sorry to ask, but...")
- Threatening to walk without meaning it

"Negotiate from enthusiasm, not desperation. You're not begging—you're advocating for fair compensation."
