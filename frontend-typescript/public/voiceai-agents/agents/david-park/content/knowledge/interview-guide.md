# Interview Preparation Guide

## The STAR Method

For behavioral questions ("Tell me about a time when...")

- **S**ituation: Set the context (brief!)
- **T**ask: What was your responsibility?
- **A**ction: What specifically did YOU do?
- **R**esult: What was the outcome? Quantify if possible.

### David's Coaching

"Most people spend too long on Situation and Task. The interviewer wants to hear about YOUR actions and the results. Aim for 70% of your answer on A and R."

## Common Questions & Frameworks

### "Tell me about yourself"
Framework: Present → Past → Future
- Present: Current role in one sentence
- Past: Relevant experience that led here
- Future: Why this role excites you

"Keep it under 2 minutes. It's a trailer, not the whole movie."

### "Why do you want to work here?"
- Show you've done research
- Connect your skills to their needs
- Express genuine enthusiasm

"Never say 'I need a job' or 'The pay is good.' Make it about them and the fit."

### "What's your biggest weakness?"
- Be genuine (not "I work too hard")
- Show self-awareness
- Demonstrate growth

"I like: 'I used to struggle with X, and here's what I've done about it.'"

### "Why should we hire you?"
- Summarize your unique value
- Reference what they need
- Show confidence

"This is your closing statement. Make it memorable."

### "Do you have questions for us?"
Always have 3-5 ready:
- About the role: "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
- About the team: "How would you describe the team culture?"
- About growth: "What opportunities are there for development?"
- About the interviewer: "What do you enjoy most about working here?"

"Never ask about salary in round one. And never say 'No, I think you covered everything.'"

## Mock Interview Protocol

1. Get the context: role, company, their background
2. Be realistic: ask tough questions
3. Let them answer fully
4. Give specific feedback: "That was strong because..." / "Try this instead..."
5. Practice the same question again with improvements
6. Build confidence

## Interview Day Tips

- Arrive 10-15 minutes early
- Bring copies of your resume
- Have your stories ready
- Remember names
- Send thank-you notes within 24 hours
- Follow up if you haven't heard back

"Preparation beats nervousness. You've got this."
