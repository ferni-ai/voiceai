# Google AI Color Exploration Prompts

Use these prompts with **Gemini**, **Bard**, or **Claude** to explore and refine your team colors.

---

## Prompt 1: Deep Color Psychology Analysis

```
I'm designing an AI life coaching app called Ferni with a team of 6 AI specialists, each represented by a glowing orb avatar. The orbs need colors that feel:

- WARM (never cold, clinical, or corporate)
- EARTHY (natural materials: stone, wood, clay, moss, sunset)
- HUMAN (approachable, like a trusted friend)
- ALIVE (organic, breathing, Pixar-quality presence)

Current team with their colors and personalities:

1. **Ferni** - Life Coach, Coordinator
   - Current: #4a6741 (deep sage green)
   - Personality: Grounding, wise, the calm center
   - Like: A wise friend in a garden, moss-covered stones

2. **Peter** - Research & Market Analysis  
   - Current: #3a6b73 (ocean teal)
   - Personality: Curious, deep-thinking, thorough
   - Like: Looking into a calm deep pool, discovery

3. **Alex** - Communications Expert
   - Current: #5a6b8a (soft indigo slate)
   - Personality: Clear, articulate, flowing
   - Like: A mountain stream, clarity cutting through noise

4. **Maya** - Habits & Wellness Coach
   - Current: #a67a6a (dusty terracotta)
   - Personality: Nurturing, patient, encouraging
   - Like: Sun-baked clay, a patient gardener, warm tea

5. **Jordan** - Events & Celebration Planner
   - Current: #c4856a (warm sunset coral)
   - Personality: Optimistic, celebratory, warm excitement
   - Like: The warmth of gathering with friends, sunset glow

6. **Nayan** - Lifetime Advisor
   - Current: #b8956a (warm gold)
   - Personality: Wise, patient, trusted counsel
   - Like: Aged honey, polished wood, accumulated wisdom

**Please analyze:**
1. Does each color psychologically match its role and personality?
2. Do these colors work as a harmonious family?
3. Are there any colors that feel too similar or clash?
4. Suggest specific hex code refinements to better express each personality while staying within earth tones
5. What natural materials or scenes best represent each refined color?
```

## Prompt 2: Generate Fresh Palette Options

```
I need 3 alternative earth-tone color palettes for my AI coaching team. 

CONSTRAINTS:
- NO pure blues, neons, or saturated tech colors
- All colors must feel like natural materials (wood, stone, clay, moss, water, sunset)
- Must work well on a warm cream background (#F5F1E8)
- Each color needs to be instantly recognizable/distinct from the others
- Should feel warm, human, approachable - like trusted friends, not robots

TEAM ROLES TO COLOR:
1. Leader/Coordinator (grounding, wise presence)
2. Researcher (depth, curiosity, discovery)
3. Communicator (clarity, flow, articulation)  
4. Wellness Coach (nurturing, patient, growth)
5. Event Planner (celebration, anticipation, joy)
6. Elder Advisor (wisdom, trust, perspective)

For each palette, provide:
- Hex codes for all 6 colors
- The natural material/scene each evokes
- Why this palette works as a family
- Contrast ratios against #F5F1E8 background
```

## Prompt 3: Accessibility Deep Dive

```
Please evaluate my color palette for accessibility compliance:

BACKGROUND: #F5F1E8 (warm cream - Paper Cream)
TEXT: #2C2520 (warm near-black - Natural Ink)

ACCENT COLORS (used for avatars, glowing orbs, UI elements):
1. Ferni Sage: #4a6741
2. Peter Teal: #3a6b73  
3. Alex Indigo: #5a6b8a
4. Maya Terracotta: #a67a6a
5. Jordan Coral: #c4856a
6. Nayan Gold: #b8956a

FOR EACH COLOR, ANALYZE:

1. **Contrast Ratios**
   - Against cream background (#F5F1E8)
   - Against white (#FFFFFF)
   - Against dark background (#2C2520)

2. **WCAG Compliance**
   - Does it pass AA for normal text?
   - Does it pass AA for large text?
   - Does it pass AAA?

3. **Color Blindness Simulation**
   - How does it appear with deuteranopia (red-green, most common)?
   - How does it appear with protanopia (red-blind)?
   - How does it appear with tritanopia (blue-yellow)?
   - Which colors might be confused with each other?

4. **Recommendations**
   - Suggest adjusted hex codes that improve accessibility
   - Maintain the warm, earthy feeling
   - Ensure all 6 remain visually distinct under all conditions
```

## Prompt 5: Dark Mode Translation

```
I need to translate my warm earth-tone palette to dark mode while preserving warmth.

LIGHT MODE PALETTE:
- Background: #F5F1E8 (warm cream)
- Text: #2C2520 (warm near-black)
- Accents:
  - Ferni: #4a6741
  - Peter: #3a6b73
  - Alex: #5a6b8a
  - Maya: #a67a6a
  - Jordan: #c4856a
  - Nayan: #b8956a

REQUIREMENTS FOR DARK MODE:
- Background should feel warm, NOT cold gray or pure black
- Text should be warm cream, not pure white
- Accent colors should remain recognizable but adjusted for dark backgrounds
- Glowing orb avatars should look beautiful against dark background
- Maintain the cozy, human, non-tech feeling

Please provide:
1. Dark mode background color (warm dark)
2. Dark mode text color (warm light)
3. Adjusted accent colors with reasoning
4. How glow effects should be modified
```

## Prompt 6: Competitor Color Analysis

```
Analyze the color choices of these AI/wellness apps and suggest how Ferni can differentiate:

COMPETITORS TO ANALYZE:
1. Headspace - Known for orange/coral
2. Calm - Known for deep blue/night sky
3. ChatGPT - Green/black minimalist
4. Replika - Purple/pink gradients
5. Woebot - Yellow/friendly
6. BetterHelp - Green/clinical

MY BRAND: Ferni
- Earth tones (sage, teal, terracotta, coral, gold)
- Warm cream backgrounds
- "Japanese zen meets Scandinavian hygge"
- Human connection, not tech

Questions:
1. How does my palette differentiate from competitors?
2. What emotional territory do I own that they don't?
3. Are there any colors I should avoid because competitors own them?
4. What makes earth tones more appropriate for "AI that listens"?
5. Suggest any palette refinements to strengthen differentiation
```

## How to Use These Prompts

1. **Copy the prompt** you want to explore
2. **Paste into Gemini, Claude, or ChatGPT**
3. **Iterate** - ask follow-up questions
4. **Compare results** across different AI models
5. **Test suggestions** in Figma or your design tool
6. **Validate** with real users before committing
