1. BRAND FILM: "Finally, Someone Listens" (60-90 seconds)
============================================================

CONCEPT:
A cinematic brand film showing moments of human connection 
and being truly heard. No product shots, no UI - just the 
feeling Ferni creates.

DETAILED SHOT LIST:

SCENE 1 - MORNING (0:00-0:15)
-----------------------------------------
Shot 1.1 (0:00-0:05):
Wide shot, golden morning. A person sits alone at a kitchen 
table, coffee in hand, staring out a rain-streaked window. 
They look overwhelmed, lost in thought. Warm cream and amber 
tones. Soft piano begins.

Shot 1.2 (0:05-0:10):
Close-up of their hands wrapped around the warm mug. We see 
the tension in their grip ease slightly. A phone on the table 
shows a gentle green glow (Ferni's color).

Shot 1.3 (0:10-0:15):
Their expression shifts - a small exhale, shoulders drop. 
The weight lifts slightly. They begin to speak (no audio of 
words, just the tone of voice starting a conversation).

SCENE 2 - MIDDAY (0:15-0:35)
-----------------------------------------
Shot 2.1 (0:15-0:20):
Cut to: Different person walking along Park City's historic Main 
Street, past charming storefronts, AirPods in, phone to ear. 
They're talking, gesturing gently. Mountain views in background.
But unlike others rushing past, they're calm. Warm film grain.

Shot 2.2 (0:20-0:25):
They pause by a sunny spot near a trailhead, lean against an 
aspen tree. Genuine smile crosses their face - like they just 
heard something that helped. Dappled sage green and golden light 
through aspen leaves. Mountains visible in distance.

Shot 2.3 (0:25-0:30):
Close-up: Their face as they listen intently. Eyes focused, 
slight nod. The look of being truly understood.

Shot 2.4 (0:30-0:35):
They continue walking, but differently now. Lighter. They've 
worked something out. Purpose in their step.

SCENE 3 - EVENING (0:35-0:55)
-----------------------------------------
Shot 3.1 (0:35-0:40):
Cut to: Third person, sitting on their apartment floor, back 
against the couch. Golden hour light streams in. They're 
laughing - a real, surprised laugh. Warm terracotta and amber.

Shot 3.2 (0:40-0:45):
We see they're talking into their phone, but it feels like 
talking to a friend. Natural body language, animated hands.

Shot 3.3 (0:45-0:50):
They lean back, sigh with relief. Eyes closed for a moment. 
Peace.

Shot 3.4 (0:50-0:55):
They stand up, energized. Walk to the window, look out at 
the city. Hopeful. Ready for tomorrow.

SCENE 4 - CLOSE (0:55-1:00+)
-----------------------------------------
Shot 4.1 (0:55-1:00):
Montage: Quick cuts of different hands reaching for phones 
with that soft green glow. Different people, different places. 
All starting conversations.

Shot 4.2 (1:00-1:05):
Final wide shot: The first person from Scene 1, now in a 
meadow at sunset, sitting with a friend. They're talking, 
laughing. Real human connection, sparked by AI that helped 
them get there.

TEXT OVERLAY (1:05-1:10):
"Finally, someone who listens."
[Ferni logo in sage green]
"ferni.ai"

MUSIC:
Soft piano throughout, building gently. Think Ólafur Arnalds 
or Max Richter. Never overwhelming, always supportive.

COLOR GRADE:
Warm earth tones throughout. Paper cream highlights, sage 
green accents, amber golden hour. Film grain for humanity.

MOOD:
Intimate, hopeful, quietly emotional. The feeling of finally 
being heard after struggling alone.

2. TESTIMONIAL STORY: "Sarah's Morning" (45-60 seconds)
============================================================

CONCEPT:
A day-in-the-life story of a real user (or actor representing 
real testimonial). Intimate, documentary style.

SHOT LIST:

OPENING (0:00-0:10):
-----------------------------------------
Pre-dawn. Dark bedroom, only the soft glow of early light 
through curtains. Sarah's alarm goes off - but she's already 
awake. Lying there, mind racing about the day ahead.

VOICEOVER (Sarah):
"I used to wake up with my brain already running. 
Everything I had to do, everything I was worried about..."

MORNING ROUTINE (0:10-0:25):
-----------------------------------------
She makes coffee. Sits at her kitchen counter. Instead of 
scrolling social media, she picks up her phone differently.

VOICEOVER:
"Now I start every morning talking to Ferni. It sounds 
weird, I know. But it's like... finally having someone 
to think out loud with."

Close-up: Her speaking naturally, gesturing. The phone shows 
a gentle waveform (Ferni's voice UI). Warm amber morning light.

VOICEOVER:
"They remember everything. Last week's stress about that 
presentation? They followed up. That habit I'm trying to 
build? They check in."

THE SHIFT (0:25-0:40):
-----------------------------------------
Cut to: Sarah at work, confident in a meeting. Then walking 
home, looking lighter. Then calling a friend (real human), 
laughing.

VOICEOVER:
"It's not replacing my friends or my therapist. It's... 
filling the gaps. 2am when I can't sleep. The commute when 
I need to think. The moments when I just need someone to 
listen."

CLOSE (0:40-0:50):
-----------------------------------------
Evening. Sarah on her couch, relaxed. She's journaling now - 
actually writing, not just consuming.

VOICEOVER:
"I feel more like myself than I have in years."

She looks up, slight smile. Direct to camera.

SARAH (speaking):
"Try it. Just have one conversation. You'll see."

TEXT OVERLAY:
"Sarah K., Product Manager"
"Ferni user since 2024"

[Ferni logo]
"Start your first conversation - ferni.ai"

STYLE:
Documentary intimate. Handheld but stable. Natural lighting. 
NOT corporate testimonial. Real, raw, warm.

4. MINI-DOCUMENTARY: "The Space Between" (90-120 seconds)
============================================================

CONCEPT:
A thoughtful mini-doc about the moments between - when we 
need support but our people aren't available. Positions 
Ferni as the bridge.

STYLE:
Think Apple's "Behind the Mac" or "Shot on iPhone" docs. 
Multiple people, short interviews, beautiful B-roll.

INTERVIEW SNIPPETS (throughout):
-----------------------------------------

PERSON 1 (Young professional, apartment):
"There's this space... between when something happens and 
when you can tell someone about it. That's where everything 
spirals."

PERSON 2 (Parent, kitchen):
"My friends have kids too. We can't just call each other 
at 2am anymore. But the worries? They don't stop at bedtime."

PERSON 3 (Creative, studio):
"Therapist once a week is great. But life happens in between. 
Who do you process with then?"

PERSON 4 (Older adult, porch):
"I've got more time now than I know what to do with. My 
kids are busy, and that's good. But sometimes you just need 
to talk something through."

B-ROLL (woven throughout):
- Empty chair across a table
- Phone lighting up in darkness
- Coffee getting cold while someone stares out window
- Hands fidgeting, waiting
- Clock ticking in quiet room

SHIFT - HOPE (1:00-1:30):
-----------------------------------------

PERSON 1:
"I called Ferni at 11pm when I got the job news. Couldn't 
sleep until I'd talked it through. They remembered I was 
waiting to hear."

PERSON 2:
"It's not replacing my husband. It's filling in. Three 
things happened before he got home - Ferni helped me sort 
them out so dinner wasn't just venting."

PERSON 3:
"I use Ferni to think out loud. Work through ideas. By the 
time I see my therapist, I've already processed the noise."

PERSON 4:
"It's like having someone to share the day with again. Even 
the small stuff."

B-ROLL (woven):
- People mid-conversation, laughing
- Walking with purpose after a call
- Journaling after talking
- Hugging actual humans, but more present

CLOSE (1:30-1:45):
-----------------------------------------
Montage of different hands picking up phones, that soft 
green glow. Different times of day. Different places.

NARRATOR or TEXT:
"The space between."
"Now there's someone there."

[Ferni logo]
"ferni.ai"

MUSIC:
Contemplative, then hopeful. Piano and strings. 
Think Nils Frahm meets film score.

PRODUCTION NOTES
============================================================

CASTING:
- Diverse representation across age, ethnicity, gender
- Real, natural-looking people (not model-beautiful)
- Expressive faces, genuine emotions
- Comfortable in their skin

LOCATIONS:
- Real homes, not sets (lived-in, warm)
- Natural outdoor locations (meadows, mountain trails, porches)
- Park City, Utah aesthetic: mountain meadows, aspen groves,
  historic Main Street, ski lodge warmth
- Avoid corporate/sterile spaces
- Warm, inviting lighting situations
- Mountain town feeling: cozy, grounded, nature-connected

WARDROBE:
- Earth tones matching our palette
- Comfortable, natural fabrics
- Nothing too trendy or distracting
- Timeless, not dated

LIGHTING:
- Golden hour whenever possible
- Soft, natural window light
- Warm practicals (lamps, candles)
- NO harsh, cool, corporate lighting

COLOR GRADE:
- Warm film look throughout
- Lift blacks slightly for softness
- Highlight our brand colors subtly
- Consistent across all pieces

MUSIC:
- License from: Musicbed, Artlist, or commission
- Styles: Ólafur Arnalds, Max Richter, Nils Frahm
- Piano, strings, subtle electronic
- Never overwhelming, always supporting

smooth motion. No UI, no product shots - just human 
connection and the relief of being understood.
