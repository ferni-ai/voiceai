Our Commitment

Accessibility

Last updated: December 5, 2025

Ferni believes that everyone deserves access to support and guidance. We're committed to making our AI life coaching platform accessible to people of all abilities, and we're continuously working to improve the experience for all users.

Voice-First Design

Ferni is built around voice interaction, making it naturally accessible to users who find typing difficult or prefer spoken communication.

Voice Input

Speak naturally to Ferni without needing to type. Just talk like you would to a friend.

Voice Output

Ferni responds with natural speech, making conversations feel human and easy to follow.

Phone Access

Call Ferni directly on the phone—no app or screen required.

Text Alternative

Prefer typing? Full text chat is available for those who want it.

Web Accessibility Standards

We strive to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards. Our website and web app include:

  • Keyboard Navigation: All interactive elements can be accessed using only a keyboard.
  • Screen Reader Support: Proper ARIA labels and semantic HTML for screen reader compatibility.
  • Color Contrast: Text and interactive elements meet minimum contrast ratios for readability.
  • Focus Indicators: Clear visual focus states for keyboard navigation.
  • Responsive Design: Works across devices and supports zoom up to 200%.
  • Reduced Motion: Respects user preferences for reduced motion when set in system settings.

Ongoing Improvements

Accessibility is an ongoing journey. We're actively working on:

  • Expanding language support for voice interactions
  • Improving compatibility with more assistive technologies
  • Adding more customization options for visual preferences
  • Regular accessibility audits and user testing

Known Limitations

We're transparent about areas where we're still improving:

  • Some complex animations may not fully respect reduced-motion preferences yet
  • Voice recognition works best with clear speech in quiet environments
  • Some third-party integrations may have their own accessibility limitations

We're actively working to address these limitations.
