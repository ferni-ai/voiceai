What's Next for Ferni

A glimpse at what we're building—and the questions we're still figuring out.

5 min read Part 8 of 8

Six months ago, Ferni didn't exist. In six months, what you see today will feel like version 0.1.

That's not hype—it's the honest pace of how this is moving. AI capabilities are advancing faster than anyone predicted. Our understanding of what works for users is deepening with every conversation. And we're shipping improvements daily.

Here's what we're excited about, what we're wrestling with, and what we're deliberately choosing not to do.

What We're Excited About

Deeper Memory

Today, Ferni remembers things you've told us explicitly. Tomorrow, Ferni will understand the patterns between things. If your mood tends to dip on Sundays, Maya might check in proactively.

Richer Specialist Interactions

Right now, specialists hand off one at a time. We're building toward something more dynamic. A conversation where Jordan jumps in to suggest a celebration while you're working with Maya on habits. More like a real team brainstorm.

Better Voice

The technical quality of voice AI is improving rapidly. Soon, talking to Ferni will feel less like "talking to AI" and more like... just talking. We're also exploring subtle differences in pacing and emphasis for each specialist.

Proactive Outreach

Right now, you come to Ferni. What if Ferni came to you? A gentle check-in before your big presentation. A nudge when you've missed a few days of your habit. Done carefully—not another annoying notification.

Integration with Your Life

Calendar, email, tasks—the tools you already use. Imagine Alex saying, "You've got three back-to-back meetings tomorrow. Want me to block some recovery time?" Privacy-first, explicit opt-in, full control.

Questions We're Wrestling With

How Much Proactivity Is Too Much?

There's a line between helpful and annoying. We're erring on the side of too little rather than too much. It's easier to add more than to recover trust after being annoying.

Should AI Have Opinions?

Right now, Ferni is largely non-directive. She asks questions. She rarely tells you what to do. But sometimes people want direction. How strongly should AI specialists express opinions?

We're experimenting with calibrating this to individual users. Some people want challenge; some want support.

How Do We Measure Success?

We know how to measure engagement. But engagement isn't our goal. Impact is.

How do we measure whether Ferni is actually helping people live better lives? We're thinking about this constantly.

What About Therapy?

Mental health need is enormous. Professional supply is limited. AI could theoretically help fill the gap.

But therapy is regulated for good reasons. We've drawn a line: Ferni is coaching, not therapy. We support wellbeing, not mental illness. If users seem to need clinical help, we suggest they seek it.

What We Won't Do

  • We won't sell your data. Ever. Your conversations are yours. Our business model is subscriptions, not advertising.
  • We won't optimize for addiction. We're designed to help you and let you go. Ideally, you finish a conversation feeling lighter and ready to engage with your actual life.
  • We won't pretend to be human. Ferni and the team are AI. That's clear and will stay clear.
  • We won't compromise on safety. If there's ever a tension between speed-to-market and user safety, safety wins. Period.

The Future Is Built One Conversation at a Time

We don't know exactly what Ferni will look like in a year. What we do know:

  • We'll keep shipping daily
  • We'll keep listening to what's working and what isn't
  • We'll keep the bar high for privacy and ethics
  • We'll keep trying to build something that genuinely helps

The future is built one conversation at a time. Thanks for being part of ours.
