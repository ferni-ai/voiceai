# What's Next for Ferni

**Subtitle**: A glimpse at what we're building—and the questions we're still figuring out.

**Reading time**: 5 minutes

---

Six months ago, Ferni didn't exist. In six months, what you see today will feel like version 0.1.

That's not hype—it's the honest pace of how this is moving. AI capabilities are advancing faster than anyone predicted. Our understanding of what works for users is deepening with every conversation. And we're shipping improvements daily.

Here's what we're excited about, what we're wrestling with, and what we're deliberately choosing not to do.

---

## What We're Excited About

### Deeper Memory

Today, Ferni remembers things you've told us explicitly. Tomorrow, Ferni will understand the patterns between things.

If you've mentioned stress about work three times in two weeks, Ferni might notice that pattern and ask about it directly. If your mood tends to dip on Sundays (the "Sunday scaries"), Maya might check in proactively.

We're not interested in creepy surveillance. We're interested in the kind of understanding a close friend develops over years—except faster, because we're paying closer attention.

### Richer Specialist Interactions

Right now, specialists hand off one at a time. You talk to Ferni, she brings in Maya, Maya hands back to Ferni.

We're building toward something more dynamic. A conversation where Jordan jumps in to suggest a celebration while you're working with Maya on habits. Where Peter adds a relevant insight without derailing the flow. More like a real team brainstorm, less like being transferred between departments.

### Better Voice

The technical quality of voice AI is improving rapidly. Latency is dropping. Expressiveness is increasing. Soon, talking to Ferni will feel less like "talking to AI" and more like... just talking.

We're also exploring ways to give each specialist a more distinct voice. Not gimmicky—not "Maya sounds like this accent"—but subtle differences in pacing, emphasis, warmth. The goal is to feel the difference, not notice it.

### Proactive Outreach

Right now, you come to Ferni. What if Ferni came to you?

Not in an intrusive way. But imagine: You mentioned you have a big presentation Thursday. Wednesday evening, Ferni sends a gentle check-in: "How are you feeling about tomorrow?"

Or: You've been tracking a habit for two weeks. Maya notices you missed a few days and reaches out: "Hey, no judgment—just wanted to see how you're doing with the morning walks."

This has to be done carefully. Nobody wants another notification. But done well, proactive support could be the difference between AI you use and AI that actually helps you grow.

### Integration with Your Life

Calendar, email, tasks—the tools you already use. What if Ferni understood your actual schedule, not just what you tell us about it?

Alex is already designed to help with communication and coordination. Imagine if she could actually see your calendar and say, "You've got three back-to-back meetings tomorrow. Want me to block some recovery time?"

We're working on integrations carefully. Privacy is paramount—you'd have to explicitly connect anything, and you'd have full control over what we can see. But the potential is significant.

---

## Questions We're Wrestling With

### How Much Proactivity Is Too Much?

There's a line between helpful and annoying. Between "I'm here when you need me" and "I'm always in your face."

We don't know exactly where that line is. It probably varies by person. We're building systems to learn individual preferences, but the defaults matter a lot. Get it wrong, and people will turn off notifications entirely, which defeats the purpose.

We're erring on the side of too little rather than too much. It's easier to add more than to recover trust after being annoying.

### Should AI Have Opinions?

Right now, Ferni is largely non-directive. She asks questions. She helps you think. She rarely tells you what to do.

But sometimes people want direction. "I don't know what to do—what would you suggest?"

How strongly should AI specialists express opinions? Should they push back on plans they think won't work? Should Maya gently challenge goals she thinks are unrealistic?

We don't have a clean answer. Pushing too hard feels presumptuous—who are we to tell you how to live? Not pushing at all feels weak—what's the point of a coach who just nods along?

We're experimenting with calibrating this to individual users. Some people want challenge; some want support. The AI should probably match what you need, not impose a one-size-fits-all approach.

### How Do We Measure Success?

We know how to measure engagement: conversation length, return visits, feature usage. But engagement isn't our goal. Impact is.

How do we measure whether Ferni is actually helping people live better lives?

Some metrics are proxies. If people keep coming back over months, not just days, that suggests real value. If people mention concrete outcomes in conversations ("I actually did the thing!"), that's a good sign.

But the true measure—are our users happier, less anxious, more fulfilled?—is hard to capture in data. We're thinking about this constantly. We don't want to optimize for engagement while missing impact.

### What About Therapy?

This is a big one. Mental health need is enormous. Professional supply is limited. AI could theoretically help fill the gap.

But therapy is regulated for good reasons. Therapeutic interventions can cause harm if done poorly. The power differential requires ethical guardrails that AI doesn't naturally have.

We've drawn a line: Ferni is coaching, not therapy. We support wellbeing, not mental illness. If users seem to need clinical help, we suggest they seek it.

But is that line in the right place? As AI capabilities grow, could AI play a bigger role in mental health support—responsibly? We don't know. We're watching the research and talking to professionals, but we're not rushing into territory that could cause harm.

---

## What We Won't Do

Some things are tempting that we're deliberately avoiding.

**We won't sell your data.** Ever. Your conversations are yours. Our business model is subscriptions, not advertising. We have no incentive to monetize your personal growth.

**We won't optimize for addiction.** Some AI companions are designed to maximize time-on-device. We're designed to help you and let you go. Ideally, you finish a conversation feeling lighter and ready to engage with your actual life.

**We won't pretend to be human.** Ferni and the team are AI. That's clear and will stay clear. Any warmth you feel is real, but the relationship is transparent.

**We won't compromise on safety.** If there's ever a tension between speed-to-market and user safety, safety wins. Period.

---

## The Future Is Built One Conversation at a Time

We don't know exactly what Ferni will look like in a year. That's partly because AI is moving so fast, and partly because our users will shape the direction.

What we do know:
- We'll keep shipping daily
- We'll keep listening to what's working and what isn't
- We'll keep the bar high for privacy and ethics
- We'll keep trying to build something that genuinely helps

If you've been following this series, thank you. We've tried to share the real story—not the sanitized pitch deck version, but the messy, thoughtful, sometimes uncertain reality of building something new.
