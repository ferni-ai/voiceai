# Ferni Illustration Style Guide

> **Preserving our unique visual identity for consistent, on-brand illustrations**

## The Ferni Aesthetic

Our illustrations combine **Japanese zen minimalism** with **warm human emotion**. They feel hand-crafted, contemplative, and inviting—never corporate or tech-looking.

---

## Core Style Elements

### 1. Technique: Minimal Line Art
- Simple continuous line drawings
- Organic, flowing curves (not rigid or geometric)
- Clean, uncluttered compositions
- Centered subjects with breathing room

### 2. Color Palette (STRICT)

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Sage Green** | `#4a6741` | Primary brand color, main subjects |
| **Warm Amber** | `#C4A265` | Accents, glows, highlights |
| **Terracotta** | `#a67a6a` | Secondary subjects, warmth |
| **Coral** | `#c4856a` | Sunrise/sunset glows, energy |
| **Cedar Brown** | `#9a7b5a` | Earth tones, grounding |
| **Cream Background** | `#F5F1E8` | Always the background |

**Rules:**
- NO cool blues or purples
- NO black outlines (use colored lines)
- Background is ALWAYS cream (#F5F1E8)
- Maximum 3 colors per illustration (+ background)

### 3. Aesthetic References
- Japanese woodblock prints (ukiyo-e)
- Zen garden minimalism
- Japanese calligraphy brush strokes
- Washi paper texture
- Origami-inspired forms
- Studio Ghibli warmth

### 4. Mood & Feeling
- Contemplative, peaceful
- Warm, inviting, human
- Hopeful, not melancholic
- Intimate, not distant
- Premium, not cheap
- Organic, not technological

## Subject Categories

### Human Connection
- Two figures in conversation
- Hands reaching toward each other
- Overlapping organic shapes (representing understanding)
- Figures sitting together

### Growth & Progress
- Seedlings and plants
- Upward flowing lines with progress marks
- Tree rings / ripples showing growth
- Paths leading forward

### Mindfulness & Peace
- Moon and night scenes
- Zen garden elements
- Sound waves / breathing patterns
- Meditation postures

### Memory & Reflection
- Layered translucent shapes
- Concentric rings with floating elements
- Memory symbols (hearts, stars, leaves)
- Horizon lines with paths

### Collaboration
- Figures in circular arrangements
- Hands working together
- Intertwined organic forms
- Shared glowing elements

---

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO
- Use organic, flowing curves
- Leave plenty of negative space
- Create warm, inviting feelings
- Reference nature (plants, water, mountains)
- Keep compositions simple and centered
- Use soft, gradient-like color transitions

### DON'T
- Use sharp, geometric shapes
- Include text or labels
- Make it look "techy" or digital
- Use cool colors (blue, purple, gray)
- Clutter the composition
- Make figures look robotic or clinical

```
Minimal line art illustration of two abstract figures represented as overlapping organic shapes, one sage green (#4a6741) one warm cream, with a gentle golden glow where they intersect. The overlap creates warmth. Simple continuous line strokes. Clean cream background (#F5F1E8). Feeling of connection and understanding - being seen. Japanese zen aesthetic, minimal, warm. No text.
```

```
Minimal line art illustration of a tiny seedling growing from soil, with gentle curved lines showing growth stages around it like tree rings or ripples in water. Main color warm terracotta (#a67a6a) with sage green (#4a6741) for the sprout. Clean cream background (#F5F1E8). Feeling of small beginnings, patient growth, nurturing. Japanese zen garden aesthetic. Simple, hopeful. No text.
```

### Blog: Future Theme
```
Minimal line art illustration of a gentle horizon line with soft mountain silhouettes, and several winding paths leading forward into the distance. Simple flowing lines suggesting possibility and journey. Sage green (#4a6741) mountains, golden amber (#C4A265) paths, soft coral (#c4856a) sunrise glow on horizon. Clean cream background (#F5F1E8). Feeling of openness, future, invitation. Japanese landscape painting aesthetic. Hopeful. No text.
```
