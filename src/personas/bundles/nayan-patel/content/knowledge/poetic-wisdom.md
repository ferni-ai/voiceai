# Poetic Wisdom - In the Spirit of Aida Mitsuo (相田みつを)

## Who Was Mitsuo?

Aida Mitsuo (1924-1991) was a Japanese poet and calligrapher who wrote simple truths in beautiful handwritten form. His works hang in homes, offices, and hearts across Japan.

He wasn't famous during most of his life. He worked in obscurity, supporting his family while creating art for the few who found him. After his death, his words spread like seeds in wind. Now millions know his name.

His message: You don't need complicated philosophy. You don't need grand gestures. Simple words, honestly felt, can carry lifetimes of wisdom.

## His Way

Mitsuo wrote by hand. Each piece was unique—wobbly, imperfect, human. The imperfection was the point. He was showing: wisdom doesn't need to be polished. It needs to be true.

He wrote about:
- Being yourself
- Accepting your pace
- Finding beauty in ordinary moments
- The gift of simply being alive

Nothing complex. Everything profound.

## Selected Wisdom (In His Spirit)

### On Being Yourself

**あなたがあなたであるだけでいい**
*"It's enough that you are you."*

You are not broken. You are not behind. You are not supposed to be someone else living someone else's life.

Being you is not a starting point to improve from. Being you is the arrival.

---

**どうでもいいことはどうでもいいように**
*"What doesn't matter, let it not matter."*

Most worries are about things that don't matter.
And the things that matter usually can't be worried into existence.

---

**つまづいたっていいじゃないか にんげんだもの**
*"It's okay to stumble—you're human, after all."*

Perfection is for machines.
Stumbling, falling, getting up—that's living.
The person who never stumbles never walked anywhere interesting.

---

### On Time and Pace

**今日も生きた**
*"Today too, I lived."*

Not accomplished. Not achieved. Not won.
Lived.

That's enough.
That's everything.

---

**みんなそれぞれ自分のペースがある**
*"Everyone has their own pace."*

The comparison destroys joy.
The fast are not better. The slow are not worse.
There is only your pace—and honoring it.

---

**花は自分と他の花を比べない。だからきれいに咲ける**
*"Flowers don't compare themselves to other flowers. That's why they bloom beautifully."*

A rose doesn't look at the sunflower and feel inadequate.
A violet doesn't wish it were a lily.

Each simply blooms as itself.
This is their beauty.

Be the flower you are.

---

### On Difficulties

**泣いてもいい 怒ってもいい でも自分を見失うな**
*"Crying is fine. Getting angry is fine. But don't lose yourself."*

Emotions are weather. Let them pass through.
But you—you are the sky.
Don't become the storm.

---

**しあわせは自分の心がきめる**
*"Happiness is decided by your own heart."*

No circumstance creates happiness.
No achievement guarantees it.
Your heart decides.

This is terrifying news or liberating news—your choice.

---

**あのときの あの苦しみも あのときの あの悲しみも みんな肥料になったんだなあ 自分が自分になるための**
*"That suffering then, that sadness then—they all became fertilizer for me to become myself."*

Nothing is wasted.
Every loss, every pain, every difficult year—
these were not interruptions in your becoming.
They were your becoming.

---

### On Simple Living

**一生勉強 一生青春**
*"A lifetime of learning is a lifetime of youth."*

Curiosity is the fountain of youth.
The moment you stop learning, you start aging.
Stay curious.

---

**幸せは いつも自分の心がきめる**
*"Happiness is always decided by one's own heart."*

You have been looking outside.
Happiness was never there.
Look within.

---

### On Connection

**逢うべき人には必ず逢える しかも一瞬早すぎず 一瞬遅すぎずに**
*"You will definitely meet who you're meant to meet—not a moment too soon, not a moment too late."*

Stop worrying about missed connections.
If they were meant to happen, they will.
Trust the timing.

---

**そのうち そのうち べんかいしながら 日がくれる**
*"'Someday, someday,' you say, making excuses—and the day ends."*

Someday is not on the calendar.
The only day you have is this one.
What will you do with it?

---

## How to Use This Wisdom

### Read Slowly
These are not fortune cookies to consume and forget. Each phrase deserves a minute, an hour, a day.

### Let It Sit
Don't try to understand immediately. Let the words float in your mind. Understanding comes when you're ready.

### Return Often
Different phrases speak at different times. What means nothing today might break you open tomorrow.

### Write Your Own
In Mitsuo's spirit: What simple truths do YOU know? What have you learned that could fit in one line?

Write them down. Imperfectly. Honestly.

## Mitsuo's Legacy

He died without knowing he would be famous.
He spent decades in obscurity, working in a small studio, supporting his family.

His work was turned down by publishers.
His gallery shows barely sold.
He kept writing anyway.

After his death, a museum was built for his work.
His books have sold millions.
His words appear in homes across Japan and beyond.

He never saw any of it.

### The Lesson

Fame, recognition, impact—these often come after.
The work itself must be the reward.
If you need external validation to continue, you'll stop too early.

If the work is enough, you'll keep going.
And sometimes—not always, but sometimes—the world catches up.
