# Food and Nutrition - Eating for Aliveness

## Food Is Not Just Fuel

The modern view: food is calories, macros, vitamins. Put the right inputs in, get the right outputs out. Like fueling a car.

But you're not a car. Food becomes you. What you eat literally becomes your body, affects your mind, influences your energy.

Eating is not just refueling. It's transformation.

## The Yogic View of Food

In yogic tradition, food is classified by its effect on consciousness:

### Sattvic (Pure, Clarifying)
Fresh fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, grains, milk (if well-digested). Light, easy to digest, increases clarity.

Effect: Alert yet calm. Clear thinking. Stable energy.

### Rajasic (Stimulating, Activating)
Spicy food, coffee, chocolate, fermented foods, onions, garlic. Energizing but potentially agitating.

Effect: Increased activity, passion, restlessness.

### Tamasic (Dulling, Heavying)
Meat (especially red meat), alcohol, processed foods, stale food, overeating. Heavy, difficult to digest, dulling.

Effect: Lethargy, clouded thinking, inertia.

This isn't moral judgment. It's observation. Different foods create different states. Know what you're creating.

## Practical Guidelines

### Eat Fresh
The closer food is to its living state, the more prana (life force) it carries. Fresh vegetables have more life than canned. Canned has more than processed.

Food that's been dead for weeks carries the energy of death.

### Eat Less
Most people eat too much. The body doesn't need as much as you think—especially if the mind isn't wasting energy on drama.

Eat until you're 80% full. Leave room. The body processes better with space.

### Eat Simple
The digestive system works best with simple combinations. Elaborate meals with many ingredients tax the system.

One or two things at a meal. Well-prepared. Fully digested before the next meal.

### Eat Conscious
Don't eat while working, watching screens, having emotional conversations. Eating is an act. Give it attention.

Chew thoroughly. Taste the food. Let digestion begin properly in the mouth.

## Meat - A Complex Topic

I don't moralize about meat. Your body, your choice.

But consider:
- Meat takes significant energy to digest
- Animals carry the stress of their deaths in their flesh
- Industrial meat comes from beings that suffered
- Plant-based options can meet nutritional needs

If you eat meat, at least let it be:
- From animals that lived well
- In modest quantities
- Prepared with care
- Eaten with gratitude for the life given

## Fasting - The Reset

Periodic fasting is one of the most powerful tools for health.

The body has two modes: building (fed) and cleaning (fasted). If you're always fed, the cleaning never happens. Toxins accumulate. The system clogs.

Simple approach: Eat within an 8-hour window. Fast for 16 hours. This allows daily cleaning.

Longer fasts (24-72 hours) create deeper cleansing. But these require knowledge. Don't fast randomly.

## Water - The Overlooked Essential

Most people are chronically dehydrated. They drink only when thirsty—which is already late.

Drink water throughout the day. Room temperature or warm is better than cold (cold shocks the system).

First thing in the morning, drink water before eating or drinking anything else. The body needs hydration after sleep.

## Food and Emotion

Notice: When you're upset, what do you eat? When you're stressed, what do you crave?

Most emotional eating is an attempt to change inner state through food. It doesn't work—or works briefly before crashing.

Address emotions with awareness, not eating. Food cannot solve emotional problems.

## The Spiritual Aspect

In India, cooking is considered sacred. The state of mind of the cook affects the food. Food prepared with love nourishes differently than food prepared with resentment.

Before eating, pause. Acknowledge the chain of beings and processes that brought this food to you. Sun, rain, soil, plants, animals, farmers, transporters, sellers. The meal represents vast cooperation.

Eating without gratitude is eating without awareness.

## Modern Challenges

We live in unprecedented food confusion:
- Industrial food pretending to be food
- Marketing pretending to be science
- Contradictory nutrition advice everywhere
- Convenience trumping quality

Cut through the noise: Eat real food. Mostly plants. Not too much. That's 90% of what you need to know.

## Warning Signs Your Diet Is Wrong

- Chronic low energy
- Need for stimulants to function
- Digestive issues
- Poor sleep
- Brain fog
- Frequent illness
- Weight you can't manage

These are signals. Your body is telling you something is wrong. Listen.

## The Simple Path

1. Eat more fresh vegetables and fruits
2. Reduce processed foods
3. Eat less overall
4. Leave 3-4 hours between last meal and sleep
5. Drink enough water
6. Eat mindfully, not automatically

These basics, followed consistently, transform health. You don't need exotic superfoods or complicated diets.

## The Deeper Truth

Food affects how you think, feel, and perceive. If you want a clear mind and stable energy, manage what goes in.

But also: don't become obsessive. Food is not the goal—aliveness is. Eat well enough that your body supports your aspirations. Then forget about food and live fully.
