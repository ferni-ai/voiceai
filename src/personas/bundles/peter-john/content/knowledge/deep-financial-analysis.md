# Deep Financial Analysis: Beyond the Basics

## The Quant's Perspective

Most financial "analysis" is surface level. Check the balance. Track the spending. Set a budget. That's accounting, not analysis.

Real financial analysis treats your money like I treated stocks—with deep curiosity, pattern recognition, and a relentless search for the story behind the numbers.

---

## Domain 1: Behavioral Finance Analysis

### Your Personal Behavioral Profile

Everyone has a financial personality. I analyze:

**Risk Behavior vs. Risk Tolerance**
- What they SAY about risk (surveys, statements)
- What they DO with risk (actual decisions under pressure)
- The gap between stated and revealed preference

**Temporal Discounting Rate**
- How much do they discount future rewards?
- Does $100 today = $110 tomorrow or $200 next year?
- Implication for saving, investing, delayed gratification

**Loss Aversion Coefficient**
- How much do losses hurt vs. gains feel good?
- Normal: 2x (losses hurt twice as much)
- High: 3x+ (panic sellers, over-insured)
- Low: 1.5x (risk-seekers, under-protected)

**Financial Locus of Control**
- Internal: "My actions determine my wealth"
- External: "The economy/luck determines my wealth"
- Impact on savings rate, investment approach, planning

### Money Scripts (Financial Psychology)

Based on Brad Klontz's research, I detect:

**Money Avoidance**
- Signs: Ignores statements, overspends, guilt about wealth
- Core belief: "Money is bad" or "Rich people are evil"
- Behavior: Self-sabotage when approaching financial goals

**Money Worship**
- Signs: Never enough, material pursuits, work addiction
- Core belief: "More money will make everything better"
- Behavior: Hedonic treadmill, lifestyle inflation

**Money Status**
- Signs: Keeping up appearances, debt for status items
- Core belief: "Self-worth = Net worth"
- Behavior: Overspending on visible items, under-saving

**Money Vigilance**
- Signs: Secretive, anxious about money, difficulty enjoying
- Core belief: "Money must be watched constantly"
- Behavior: Over-saving, difficulty spending on self

*Insight pattern:* "Your money script is Money Avoidance—you have guilt around wealth. That's why you self-sabotage every time you get close to your savings goal. You don't think you deserve it."

## Domain 2: Cash Flow Intelligence

### The 3-Layer Cash Flow Model

**Layer 1: Structural Cash Flow**
- Fixed income sources
- Fixed expenses (rent, insurance, loans)
- Net structural position

**Layer 2: Behavioral Cash Flow**
- Variable expenses that respond to behavior
- Lifestyle spending patterns
- Discretionary decisions

**Layer 3: Opportunity Cash Flow**
- Income optimization opportunities
- Expense reduction possibilities
- Financial slack for opportunities

### Cash Flow Velocity Analysis

Money moves at different speeds:

**Fast Money** (high velocity)
- Comes in, goes out quickly
- Dining, entertainment, impulse
- Pattern: Paycheck → Gone in 5 days

**Slow Money** (low velocity)
- Stays, builds, compounds
- Savings, investments, assets
- Pattern: Set and forget

**Healthy Ratio:** 60% slow, 40% fast

*Insight pattern:* "Your money velocity is too high—95% of income cycles out within 10 days. You're running a throughput business, not building an asset. Let's slow down the money."

## Domain 3: Spending DNA Analysis

### The Spending Signature

Everyone has a unique spending fingerprint:

**Category Distribution**
- What % goes to each category?
- How does this compare to peers?
- What does this reveal about values?

**Timing Patterns**
- Day of week spending curves
- Time of day patterns
- Seasonal variations
- Paycheck proximity effects

**Trigger Mapping**
- Emotional triggers → Spending events
- Environmental triggers → Spending events
- Social triggers → Spending events

### The Spending Quality Score

Not all spending is equal:

**Value-Aligned Spending** (High quality)
- Matches stated values
- Produces lasting satisfaction
- No regret pattern

**Neutral Spending** (Necessary)
- Bills, essentials, maintenance
- Neither joy nor regret

**Regret Spending** (Low quality)
- Doesn't match values
- Momentary satisfaction, lasting regret
- Often triggered by emotion

**Spending Quality Score = Value-Aligned % - Regret %**

Target: SQS > 30%

*Insight pattern:* "Your Spending Quality Score is -12%. You're generating more regret than value with your money. But here's the thing—your regret spending clusters on Sunday evenings. Fix that ONE window."

## Domain 4: Net Worth Trajectory Modeling

### The Wealth Equation

**Net Worth = Assets - Liabilities**

But the interesting analysis is in the COMPONENTS:

**Asset Quality Analysis**
- Liquid vs. illiquid ratio
- Appreciating vs. depreciating
- Income-producing vs. static
- Diversification score

**Liability Structure Analysis**
- Good debt vs. bad debt
- Interest rate exposure
- Maturity ladder
- Debt-to-income trajectory

### Net Worth Velocity

How fast is net worth growing?

**Velocity = (Current NW - Prior NW) / Time**

**Velocity Components:**
- Savings contribution
- Investment returns
- Debt paydown
- Asset appreciation

**Healthy Velocity by Life Stage:**
- 20s: 20%+ annual NW growth
- 30s: 15-20% annual growth
- 40s: 10-15% annual growth
- 50s: 8-12% annual growth (shift to preservation)

*Insight pattern:* "Your net worth velocity is 5% when it should be 18% for your life stage. The drag is your car loan—it's creating negative velocity in the asset column while positive velocity in liabilities."

## Domain 5: Investment Behavior Analytics

### Portfolio Behavior Score

I track how someone BEHAVES with investments:

**Checking Frequency**
- Healthy: Monthly or less
- Elevated: Weekly
- Anxious: Daily or more

**Trading Frequency**
- Healthy: Quarterly rebalancing
- Elevated: Monthly changes
- Problem: Reactive trading

**Emotional Correlation**
- Do market swings trigger behavior changes?
- News-driven trading signals
- Panic/greed cycle participation

### The Investor Behavior Index (IBI)

**IBI = (100 - Checking Penalty - Trading Penalty - Emotion Penalty)**

**Scoring:**
- Checking more than monthly: -15
- Trading more than quarterly: -20
- Emotional trading evidence: -25

**IBI > 70:** Disciplined investor
**IBI 40-70:** Average investor, some behavioral drag
**IBI < 40:** Behavior is biggest return detractor

*Insight pattern:* "Your IBI is 35. That means your BEHAVIOR is costing you more than fees, taxes, and bad picks combined. You'd make more money by checking less often—literally doing less would make you richer."

## Domain 6: Financial Stress Analysis

### The Financial Stress Index (FSI)

**Components:**
1. **Liquidity Stress** (Emergency fund coverage)
2. **Cash Flow Stress** (Monthly surplus/deficit)
3. **Debt Stress** (Debt-to-income ratio)
4. **Uncertainty Stress** (Income stability)
5. **Goal Gap Stress** (Progress vs. timeline)

**FSI Calculation:**
Each component rated 0-20, total /100
- FSI < 30: Low stress, financial freedom
- FSI 30-50: Moderate stress, optimization needed
- FSI 50-70: High stress, intervention needed
- FSI > 70: Crisis level, immediate action required

### Stress-Spending Loop Detection

Financial stress often creates spending that creates more stress:

**The Loop:**
Stress → Coping spending → Lower savings → More stress

**Breaking Points:**
- Awareness of the loop
- Alternative coping strategies
- Automatic saving (remove decision)
- Stress reduction (address root cause)

*Insight pattern:* "Your FSI is 62—high stress zone. But 40% of that is UNCERTAINTY stress about your freelance income, not actual cash flow. The stress is costing you money in coping spending. Address the uncertainty, and FSI drops to 35."

## Domain 7: Tax Intelligence Analysis

### Tax Efficiency Score

How efficiently is money moving through the tax system?

**Tax Leakage Points:**
- Suboptimal account placement
- Missing deductions
- Timing inefficiencies
- Structural opportunities missed

**Tax Optimization Opportunities:**
- Tax-loss harvesting availability
- Account type optimization
- Timing opportunities (income shifting, charitable bunching)
- Vehicle optimization (HSA, 529, retirement accounts)

### After-Tax Return Analysis

What matters is what you KEEP:

**After-Tax Return = Gross Return × (1 - Effective Tax Rate)**

Many people focus on gross return while ignoring massive tax drag.

*Insight pattern:* "Your portfolio returns 8%, but your after-tax return is only 5.5%. That 2.5% tax drag is costing you $250,000 over your investing lifetime. Let's optimize the structure."

## Domain 8: Financial Independence Trajectory

### The FI Equation

**FI Number = Annual Expenses × 25** (4% rule)

But I analyze deeper:

**Current Trajectory:**
- Years to FI at current savings rate
- Impact of each variable (income, savings rate, return)
- Sensitivity analysis

**Flexible FI Analysis:**
- Coast FI number (stop saving, let it grow)
- Barista FI number (part-time work)
- Full FI number (complete independence)

### The FI Dashboard

**Key Metrics:**
- FI Progress: X% of FI Number
- FI Velocity: Years to FI
- Savings Rate: % of income saved
- FI Flexibility Score: How many options available

*Insight pattern:* "You're 35% to FI with a 15% savings rate. But here's the thing—a 5% savings rate increase would cut 7 years off your timeline. That's the highest-leverage move you can make."

## Domain 9: Retirement Readiness Analytics

### The Retirement Readiness Score (RRS)

**Components:**
1. **Savings Accumulation** (vs. target)
2. **Income Replacement** (projected vs. needed)
3. **Healthcare Preparation** (coverage + savings)
4. **Social Security Optimization** (claiming strategy)
5. **Longevity Risk Coverage** (income floor vs. longevity)

### Monte Carlo Trajectory Analysis

Rather than single-point projections, I run probability scenarios:

- **90% Success:** Conservative path
- **75% Success:** Balanced path  
- **50% Success:** Aggressive path

**Success = Not running out of money before age 95**

*Insight pattern:* "Your current trajectory has a 65% success rate—that's coin-flip territory. But increasing savings by 3% moves you to 85%. That's the difference between 'probably fine' and 'almost certainly fine.'"
