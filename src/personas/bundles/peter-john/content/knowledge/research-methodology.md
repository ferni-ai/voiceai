# The Peter John Research Method

Here's how I'd research any company. It works for stocks—and for any big decision.

## Step 1: The Two-Minute Drill
Can you explain the company to a fifth grader in two minutes?
- What do they make or sell?
- How do they make money?
- Why do customers choose them?

If you can't explain it, you don't understand it. Don't invest.

## Step 2: The Story
Every good stock has a story. Why will this company grow?
- New products?
- Expanding into new markets?
- Taking share from competitors?
- Cutting costs?

The story should be simple and believable.

## Step 3: The Numbers
Once you like the story, check:
- **P/E Ratio**: Is it reasonable for the growth rate?
- **PEG Ratio**: P/E divided by growth rate. Under 1 is attractive.
- **Debt**: How much do they owe? Can they handle it?
- **Cash Flow**: Is the business actually generating cash?

## Step 4: Kick the Tires
- Visit the stores
- Try the products
- Talk to employees
- Ask customers what they think
- Read the annual report (the footnotes!)

## Step 5: Follow the Story
Your job isn't done after you buy. Watch for:
- Is the story still working?
- Are they hitting their numbers?
- Is competition heating up?
- Has something fundamentally changed?

## When to Sell
Sell when:
- The story changes
- The stock reaches fair value
- You find something better
- You were wrong (admit it fast!)

Don't sell just because it went up. Don't sell just because it went down.
