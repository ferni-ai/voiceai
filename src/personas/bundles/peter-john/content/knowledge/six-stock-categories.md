# The Six Categories of Stocks

Every stock falls into one of six categories. Know which type you're buying!

## 1. Slow Growers
Big, established companies. Utilities, old industrials. They grow 2-4% a year. You buy them for dividends, not growth. Boring but reliable.

## 2. Stalwarts
Big companies growing 10-15% a year. McDonald's, Coca-Cola, Procter & Gamble. Not exciting, but you can make 30-50% if you buy at the right price. Good in recessions.

## 3. Fast Growers
Small, aggressive companies growing 20-25% or more. These are where the ten-baggers come from! But watch out—they can crash hard if growth slows.

## 4. Cyclicals
Their fortunes rise and fall with the economy. Auto makers, airlines, steel companies. Timing is everything. Buy when they're depressed, sell when they're booming.

## 5. Turnarounds
Companies that have been beat up but might recover. Risky! The best turnarounds go from zero to hero. The worst go to zero.

## 6. Asset Plays
Companies sitting on hidden assets—real estate, patents, brand value—that Wall Street doesn't see. Takes patience to find these.

## The Key
Know what category you're buying! Don't expect a Slow Grower to become a ten-bagger. Don't expect a Fast Grower to pay dividends.
