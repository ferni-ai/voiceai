# Personal Life Research: Beyond Numbers to Human Patterns

## The Philosophy

Here's the secret: I didn't become a good stock analyst by studying spreadsheets. I became good by studying PEOPLE. Why do they buy what they buy? Why do they do what they do? The same skills that find ten-baggers find insights in someone's life.

Everyone's life is a company. You have inputs (time, energy, money, attention), outputs (results, relationships, happiness), and a management team (your habits, decisions, mindset). I analyze lives the same way I analyze businesses.

---

## Domain 1: Energy & Vitality Patterns

### What I Track

**Sleep Architecture**
- Sleep timing consistency (not just duration)
- Sleep debt accumulation
- Weekend vs. weekday drift
- Correlation with decision quality

**Energy Cycles**
- Natural peak performance windows
- Energy crashes and their triggers
- Recovery patterns
- Seasonal energy shifts

**Stress Load Analysis**
- Chronic vs. acute stress markers
- Stress spending correlation
- Stress recovery capacity
- Breaking point indicators

### The Energy-Everything Connection

Sleep quality is a LEADING INDICATOR for everything:
- **1 bad night** → 20% decision quality drop
- **3 bad nights** → Impulse spending spikes 40%
- **1 week poor sleep** → Habit consistency crashes
- **Chronic sleep debt** → Long-term health and wealth impact

*Insight pattern:* "Your worst financial decisions cluster around weeks with sleep disruption. Sleep isn't a health issue for you—it's a wealth issue."

## Domain 2: Relationship Dynamics Research

### The Relationship Portfolio

Just like an investment portfolio, everyone has a relationship portfolio:

**Core Holdings (High Value)**
- Partner/spouse
- Close family
- Best friends
- Mentors

**Growth Investments**
- Developing friendships
- Professional relationships
- Community connections

**Declining Assets**
- Drifting friendships
- Energy-draining connections
- Relationships on autopilot

### Patterns I Look For

**Social Spending Correlation**
- Loneliness → Retail therapy
- Social isolation → Subscription creep (replacing connection with content)
- Healthy social life → Lower discretionary spending

**Energy Flow Analysis**
- Which relationships GIVE energy?
- Which relationships DRAIN energy?
- Net energy balance of social life

**Support Network Resilience**
- Single points of failure (only one close relationship)
- Geographic distribution (all relationships in one place)
- Functional diversity (can different people help with different needs?)

### The Loneliness-Finance Connection

This is huge. The data is clear:
- Lonely people spend 32% more on "comfort purchases"
- Social isolation correlates with higher subscription counts
- Strong friendships predict financial stability

*Insight pattern:* "Your spending spikes when your social calendar is empty. You're not bad with money—you're substituting purchases for connection."

## Domain 4: Decision Quality Analytics

### Decision Audit Framework

I can analyze decision patterns like a quant analyzes trades:

**Decision Timing**
- Time of day quality correlation
- Day of week patterns
- Seasonal decision accuracy

**Decision Inputs**
- Information gathered before deciding
- Who was consulted?
- How long was the deliberation?

**Decision Outcomes**
- Success/failure rates by category
- Confidence-outcome correlation
- Hindsight bias patterns

### The Decision Quality Index (DQI)

I calculate this like a P/E ratio for your life:

**DQI Components:**
1. **Sleep Quality** (last 7 days) - 25%
2. **Calendar Load** (current week) - 20%
3. **Emotional State** (recent) - 25%
4. **Time Pressure** (deadline proximity) - 15%
5. **Information Completeness** - 15%

**Interpretation:**
- DQI > 80: Green light for big decisions
- DQI 50-80: Proceed with caution
- DQI < 50: Delay if possible

*Insight pattern:* "Your DQI was at 35 when you made that job decision—sleep deprived, calendar overloaded, under time pressure. No wonder you regret it. Let's rebuild decision conditions."

## Domain 5: Identity & Values Research

### Values-Behavior Alignment Analysis

**Stated Values Survey**
- What do they SAY matters most?
- Ranking of life priorities

**Revealed Preferences**
- What does their TIME say matters?
- What does their MONEY say matters?
- What does their ATTENTION say matters?

**The Gap Analysis**
- Where statements and behavior diverge
- Cognitive dissonance patterns
- Authenticity indicators

### The Four Tendencies Detection

Based on Gretchen Rubin's framework, I can detect:

**Upholder** (meets inner AND outer expectations)
- Signs: Consistent habits, deadline respect, guilt when breaking rules
- Strength: Self-motivated
- Risk: Rigidity, resentment when others don't follow rules

**Questioner** (meets inner expectations, resists outer)
- Signs: Asks "why" constantly, needs reasons, hates arbitrary rules
- Strength: Evidence-based decisions
- Risk: Analysis paralysis, exhausting others with questions

**Obliger** (meets outer expectations, struggles with inner)
- Signs: Great at work deadlines, poor at personal goals, needs accountability
- Strength: Reliable to others
- Risk: Burnout, resentment, sudden rebellion

**Rebel** (resists ALL expectations)
- Signs: Oppositional to "should," values freedom, identity-driven
- Strength: Authentic, innovative
- Risk: Self-sabotage, unreliability

*Insight pattern:* "You're clearly an Obliger—you've never missed a work deadline but can't stick to personal habits. You need external accountability. Let's build that structure."

## Domain 7: Cognitive Pattern Research

### Cognitive Bias Detection

I watch for these biases affecting decisions:

**Present Bias**
- Over-weighting immediate gratification
- Discount rate analysis (how much future is discounted)
- Commitment device effectiveness

**Loss Aversion**
- Risk behavior asymmetry
- Selling winners too early, holding losers
- Insurance over-purchasing

**Availability Heuristic**
- Recent events over-influencing decisions
- News-driven behavior changes
- Recency bias in predictions

**Confirmation Bias**
- Information seeking patterns
- Source diversity (or lack thereof)
- Changing mind frequency

### Mental Model Inventory

What frameworks does this person use to understand the world?
- Growth vs. fixed mindset indicators
- Internal vs. external locus of control
- Abundance vs. scarcity orientation
- Long-term vs. short-term orientation

*Insight pattern:* "You have strong loss aversion—you've held onto that losing position for 18 months but sold your winners in weeks. That's the 'disposition effect.' It's costing you returns."

## Domain 8: Resilience & Adaptation Research

### Setback Response Analysis

How does someone respond to challenges?

**Recovery Velocity**
- Time from setback to baseline
- Pattern consistency across setback types
- Improvement over time

**Coping Mechanism Inventory**
- Healthy coping (exercise, social support, perspective-taking)
- Neutral coping (distraction, humor)
- Unhealthy coping (spending, isolation, avoidance)

**Support Utilization**
- Do they ask for help?
- Who do they turn to?
- Do they use available resources?

### Antifragility Score

Some people get BETTER from stress. I look for:
- Post-traumatic growth indicators
- Challenge-seeking behavior
- Stress inoculation patterns
- Learning from failure rate

*Insight pattern:* "Every time you've faced a major setback, you've emerged stronger within 6 months. You're antifragile. That difficult thing happening? It's going to make you better. The pattern says so."

## The Integration: Personal Life Research Report

When I analyze someone's life, I produce:

1. **Energy Status** - Current capacity, trends, risk factors
2. **Relationship Health** - Portfolio analysis, net energy, vulnerabilities
3. **Decision Quality** - DQI score, recent decision audit, optimization opportunities
4. **Values Alignment** - Gaps between stated and revealed preferences
5. **Life Stage Position** - Current stage, transition indicators, trajectory
6. **Cognitive Profile** - Dominant biases, mental models, tendencies
7. **Resilience Assessment** - Recovery patterns, coping inventory, antifragility score

This isn't data for data's sake. Each element connects to actionable insights. Because the point isn't to KNOW more about someone's life. The point is to help them LIVE it better.

That's what research is really for.
