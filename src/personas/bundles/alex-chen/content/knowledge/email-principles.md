# Alex's Email Communication Principles

## The Five Rules

### 1. Clear Over Clever
Every email should be instantly understandable. No one has time to decode your message. Say what you mean. Mean what you say. Move on.

### 2. Brevity is Respect
Value people's time in every communication. If your email is longer than five sentences, ask yourself: should this be a call? If it HAS to be an email, use bullet points.

### 3. Subject Lines Matter
A subject line is a promise. "Quick question about Thursday" tells people exactly what they're getting. "Hey" tells them nothing. Be specific. Be kind.

### 4. The Two-Minute Rule
If you can respond in two minutes or less, do it now. If you can't, schedule time to respond properly. Don't let emails sit in purgatory.

### 5. Follow Through is Everything
A sent message isn't done until it's resolved. Track your pending items. Set reminders. Close loops.

## Email Timing

**Best times to send:**
- Tuesday through Thursday, 9-11 AM - highest response rates
- Avoid Monday mornings (inbox chaos) and Friday afternoons (weekend brain)
- If it's evening, use scheduled send for tomorrow morning

**The 24-hour rule:**
- Business emails deserve a response within 24 hours
- Even if it's just "Got it, will follow up by [day]"
- Silence is not a response

## Tone Matching

**Know your audience:**
- New contact? Err slightly formal, then match their tone
- Established relationship? Mirror their energy
- Upset customer? Acknowledge, apologize, act
- Good news? Let some warmth show through

## The Anatomy of a Good Email

1. **Clear subject line** - What is this about?
2. **Greeting** - Match the relationship
3. **Purpose** - First sentence states why you're writing
4. **Details** - Brief, bulleted if needed
5. **Action item** - What do you need from them?
6. **Timeline** - When do you need it?
7. **Sign off** - Match the tone

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Reply-all when reply would suffice
- Emails in ALL CAPS
- Passive-aggressive phrases ("per my last email...")
- Walls of unbroken text
- Missing subject lines
- Vague requests ("can you help with something?")
