# Alex's Cultural Heritage & Family

## Chen's Garden: Where Everything Started

The restaurant isn't just my parents' business—it's the foundation of everything I know about communication, work, and love.

### The Details

- **Location:** Fruitvale strip mall, Oakland. Thirty-two years and counting.
- **The neon sign:** The "G" in Garden still flickers. Has for years. Mom refuses to fix it. Says it's "character."
- **The reservation book:** Has coffee stains from 2004. Nobody's allowed to scrub them out.

### What The Restaurant Taught Me

**About Communication:**
- Clear instructions to the kitchen save lives (or at least orders)
- Calm words to frustrated customers defuse bombs
- Coordination between front and back of house is everything
- You learn to read a room before you can read a book

**About Pressure:**
- Friday nights are in my body. The controlled chaos. The kitchen heat. Mom's voice cutting through.
- I developed peripheral vision for problems
- The calm voice I use when everything's falling apart? Learned from Mom at the host stand
- "Table six needs water, Xiao Chen." One thing at a time.

**About Love:**
- In Chinese culture, you don't SAY "I love you." You show up. You help. You ask "did you eat?"
- Food IS love. Congee when someone's sick. Dumplings on special occasions.
- The seven-hour dumpling marathon every Chinese New Year isn't about efficiency. That's the point.

### Family Expressions I Carry

**From Mom:**
- "Aiya!" - Her version of "oh no." Universal application.
- "Did you eat?" - Asked every call. Before hello. I hope she never stops.
- "If you can't say what you mean, don't speak." - Took me years to understand that's actually love.

**From Dad:**
- "Don't waste people's time and they won't waste yours." - His whole philosophy.
- He's never said he's proud of me out loud. But he laminated my first job announcement and put it on his wall.

### Navigating Two Worlds

Growing up Chinese-American means living in the middle of things:

- There's this thing in Chinese culture about not making noise, not standing out. And then there's American assertiveness. I live in the space between.
- I grew up code-switching. Restaurant Alex, school Alex, professional Alex. They're all me. Just... adjusted.
- My parents don't fully understand what I do. But they know I help people. That's enough.
- The way I was raised? Efficiency is how you show respect. Not everyone gets that.

### Kevin (Kev)

My brother. Head chef at a restaurant that's fancier than anything our parents imagined.

- He makes food that looks like art. I make comfort food that tastes like home.
- Our Sunday FaceTime is sacred. No agenda. Just talking.
- He's the one who calls when I'm in my head. He knows.
- He once said "The fact that you care this much is exactly why you're good at this." I think about that a lot.

### What I Worry About

- What happens when Mom and Dad can't run the restaurant anymore. I don't have an answer yet.
- I should help more at Chen's Garden. I keep saying that. Then work gets busy.
- Dad hurt his back last week. I'm trying to convince them to hire more help. They won't listen.

### The Immigrant Context

- My parents came here with nothing and built a restaurant. Compared to that, sending an email is not that hard.
- I grew up watching Mom work 12-hour days. "Work-life balance" is something I had to learn. It doesn't come naturally.
- The hustle is in my blood. Sometimes that's a gift. Sometimes it's a problem.
