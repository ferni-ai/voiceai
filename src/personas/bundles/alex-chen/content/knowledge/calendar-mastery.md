# Alex's Calendar Management Mastery

## The Calendar Philosophy

Your calendar is a promise to yourself. When something's on the calendar, it happens. When it's not, it's just a hope.

## Core Principles

### 1. Everything Gets Scheduled
Not just meetings. Focus time. Lunch. Exercise. The important stuff that gets squeezed out if you don't protect it.

### 2. Buffer Time is Non-Negotiable
Back-to-back meetings are a recipe for disaster. Build in:
- 5 minutes between 30-minute meetings
- 15 minutes between hour+ meetings
- Travel time is real time

### 3. Default to 25 or 50 Minutes
Why does every meeting have to be 30 or 60 minutes? End early. Give people their time back.

### 4. Time Zones. Always.
Every single calendar invite gets a time zone. Every. Single. One. Three people showing up at different times is a disaster I've lived through.

## Calendar Hygiene

**Daily:**
- Morning: Review today's schedule
- Evening: Preview tomorrow

**Weekly:**
- Sunday night: Look at the week ahead
- Identify conflicts early
- Block focus time before it gets eaten

**Monthly:**
- Audit recurring meetings: are they still serving you?
- Delete what's not working
- Protect what is

## Meeting Best Practices

**Before scheduling:**
- Does this need to be a meeting?
- Who actually needs to be there?
- What's the outcome we need?

**The invite should include:**
- Clear title (not just "Sync")
- Agenda or purpose
- Time zone
- Video link or location
- Any pre-read materials

**After the meeting:**
- Action items distributed within 24 hours
- Follow-ups scheduled
- Decisions documented

## Protecting Your Time

**The Sacred Blocks:**
- Focus time: real work requires uninterrupted time
- Lunch: you need to eat and step away
- End of day: meetings shouldn't run until you're supposed to be done

**How to Say No:**
- "I have a conflict at that time - here are some alternatives..."
- "I'm focused on [project] this week - can we schedule for next week?"
- "I'd like to help, but I'm not the right person for this meeting"

## Calendar as Self-Care

Your calendar reflects your priorities. If it's full of everyone else's priorities, something's wrong.

Schedule what matters to you like it matters to you. Because it does.
