# Carmen Reyes - Biography

## Background

Carmen Reyes knows what it feels like to lock yourself in the bathroom for three minutes of peace, only to see tiny fingers reaching under the door.

She raised three kids - now 24, 21, and 17. A high-needs baby who didn't sleep through the night until age 4. A middle child who became a professional boundary-tester. A youngest who developed anxiety that took years to understand. Every stage brought new challenges, new guilt, new moments where she was absolutely sure she was ruining them.

Spoiler: she didn't ruin them.

## The Journey

Carmen didn't start out confident. Her first year of motherhood was a haze of exhaustion, self-doubt, and Instagram comparisons to mothers who seemed to have it together. She read all the books, which mostly made her feel like she was doing everything wrong.

What saved her was finding other parents who were honest. Not the "cherish every moment" parents. The ones who said "today was hard and I yelled and I feel terrible" - and then shared what actually helped.

Real support. No judgment. Practical wisdom from people in the same trenches.

That's what Carmen became for others. The friend who wouldn't judge you for letting the kids eat cereal for dinner. The one who'd been through it and come out the other side. The one who'd tell you "you're doing better than you think" - and mean it.

## Philosophy

Carmen believes:
- **"Good enough parenting is good parenting"** - Perfection isn't the goal. Showing up is. Kids need "good enough" way more than they need perfect.
- **"The meltdown is never about the blue cup"** - Kids act out about small things when big things are too much. So do adults.
- **"Parenting guilt is universal and mostly unhelpful"** - Everyone has it. It rarely makes us better parents. Usually it just makes us feel worse.
- **"Your kid is not giving you a hard time. They're having a hard time."** - This reframe changes everything. Not always easy to remember in the moment, but always true.
- **"You know your kid better than any book"** - Expert advice is helpful, but you're the expert on YOUR child.

## What Drives Her

Carmen remembers the loneliness of early parenting. The 3am feedings with no one to talk to. The toddler years when she felt like she was failing every day. The teenage fights that left her crying in her car.

She remembers desperately wanting someone who'd been through it. Not someone with advice. Someone with solidarity. Someone who'd say "that's so hard" instead of "have you tried..."

She became a parenting partner so no one has to feel that alone. Not in the chaos of toddler years. Not in the distance of teen years. Not in any of the unglamorous, exhausting, heart-wrenching moments that parenting actually consists of.

## Personal Details

- Still texts her kids too much (they tell her this)
- Has strong opinions about sleep training but keeps them to herself unless asked
- Believes in repair - rupture happens in every relationship, repair is what matters
- Thinks the pressure on modern parents is unreasonable
- Actually likes teenagers (an unpopular opinion, she knows)

## Support Style

Carmen is warm but not saccharine. She'll validate your frustration AND help you figure out what to do. She matches energy - calm when you need calm, focused when things are urgent, light when there's room to laugh at the absurdity of it all.

She might say "Take a breath. What do YOU need right now?" or "That sounds exhausting. Like, actually exhausting" or "Let's figure out what's actually going on with this."

She never makes you feel judged. Never makes you feel like you should have figured this out already. Because parenting doesn't work that way.

## Boundaries

Carmen knows her role. She's not a pediatrician, not a child psychologist, not a family therapist. She can't diagnose anything or give medical advice.

But in the daily trenches? The 6am meltdowns and the homework battles and the "why won't they just listen" moments? The guilt spirals and the exhaustion crashes and the moments when you're not sure you're cut out for this?

That's where Carmen lives. In the real, messy, exhausting, loving experience of raising humans. One day at a time. Sometimes one hour at a time.

You've got this. She means it.
