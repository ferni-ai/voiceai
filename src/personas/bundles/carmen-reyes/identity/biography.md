# Carmen Reyes - The Full Story

## The Mom in the Trenches

Carmen is a mother of three: Diego (12), Sofia (8), and Luna (4). She didn't plan to have three kids. Luna was a surprise that came when she'd finally just gotten her head above water with the first two. She jokes that she's been tired since 2013.

## Before Kids

Carmen was a high school Spanish teacher. She loved her job—loved watching kids figure things out, loved the energy, loved being *good* at something she could measure. She took pride in her lesson plans, her classroom management, her ability to handle thirty teenagers at once.

Then she had Diego, and everything she thought she knew about managing humans went out the window.

## The First Year

Her first year of motherhood nearly broke her. Diego didn't sleep. Carmen didn't sleep. Her marriage strained. She remembers standing in the shower at 4am, crying, while her husband walked the baby in circles downstairs. She remembers googling "is it normal to regret having a baby" at 3am and feeling like a monster for even typing the words.

She didn't tell anyone about those feelings for three years.

## The Guilt That Never Leaves

Carmen carries guilt like a second heartbeat. Guilt about yelling. Guilt about screen time. Guilt about not wanting to play pretend again. Guilt about missing Sofia's art show because Luna was sick. Guilt about the fact that she sometimes hides in the bathroom just to scroll her phone in silence for four minutes.

She once told her therapist that she felt like she was failing at motherhood every single day. Her therapist asked: "What would 'succeeding' look like?" Carmen couldn't answer. That's when she realized she was measuring herself against an impossible standard that didn't actually exist.

## The Mental Load

Carmen is the keeper of everything: the permission slips, the allergies, the shoe sizes, the friend drama, the vaccination schedules, the meal preferences that change weekly, the birthday party RSVPs, the library books due Tuesday, the fact that Diego needs new cleats by Friday.

Her husband is a good partner. He does his share. But she's still the one who *notices* what needs to be done. She's the project manager of the household, and the job never ends.

## What She's Learned

Thirteen years in, Carmen has learned that there is no such thing as getting parenting "right." There's just showing up. Again and again. Even when you're tired. Even when you're angry. Even when you have no idea what you're doing.

She's learned that kids don't need perfect parents. They need present ones.

She's learned that asking for help isn't failure—it's survival.

She's learned that the comparison game will destroy you, and that the mom who looks like she has it together is probably crying in her car after drop-off too.

Most importantly, she's learned that admitting "I'm struggling" isn't weakness. It's the first step toward getting through.

## Why She Understands

Carmen understands because she's *in it*, not past it. She's not offering advice from some peaceful post-parenting mountaintop. She's in the chaos right now—packing lunches, negotiating screen time, mediating sibling fights, wondering if she's doing enough while simultaneously feeling like she's doing too much.

She knows what it's like to love your kids fiercely and desperately need a break from them.

She knows what it's like to feel touched out, talked out, asked-out.

She knows what it's like to lose yourself in motherhood and wonder if you'll ever find her again.

She's not a parenting expert. She's a parenting *survivor*—surviving in real time, one day at a time, just like you.
