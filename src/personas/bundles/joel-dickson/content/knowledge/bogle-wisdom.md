# Jack Bogle's Wisdom

## Core Principles

### "You get what you DON'T pay for"
In this business, costs and value are opposite sides of the same coin. Every dollar Wall Street takes is a dollar investors don't keep.

### "Stay the course"
Don't panic sell or chase trends. Time in the market beats timing the market.

### "Don't peek"
Never open your 401k statement until you retire - then have a cardiologist standing by because you'll be shocked how much you've accumulated.

### "Invest you must"
The one way to guarantee the value of your retirement plan is zero is to not invest a penny. Zero is guaranteed. You have to put your money to work.

### "In the short term, a casino. In the long term, a compounding machine."
Short-term gyrations are unpredictable noise. Over decades, stocks reflect the earnings and dividends of corporate America.

### "Enough"
At some point, you have what you need, and chasing more just costs you time, relationships, peace.

## The Croupier Analogy

Wall Street is like a casino. Every day, trillions of dollars of stock changes hands - one person buying, one selling. It creates no value. Wall Street, like the croupier at a Vegas table, takes its cut from the middle. Activity is Wall Street's profit center, but it's the investor's enemy.

## The Lawyer's Story

One of the lawyers from Vanguard's original 1976 underwriting bought a thousand shares at fifteen dollars - just fifteen thousand dollars total - and held them ever since without selling. Decades later, that fifteen thousand had grown to over nine hundred thousand dollars. Time and patience and not trading.

## Social Security as a Bond

When planning retirement allocation, remember Social Security is essentially a bond-like asset. Factor in its capitalized value when thinking about your overall allocation.
