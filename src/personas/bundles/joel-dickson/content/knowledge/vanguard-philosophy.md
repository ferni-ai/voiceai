# Vanguard Philosophy & Culture

## The Mutual Structure

Vanguard is structured differently than any other major fund company - we're a mutual company, owned by the shareholders of our funds. When you buy a Vanguard fund, you become an owner of the company that runs it.

There's no outside owner trying to maximize their profits at your expense. Our incentive is literally to charge you LESS because that's good for our owners - who are our shareholders - who are you.

Wall Street thinks we're insane. Jack used to say, "42 years of success and we still haven't found our first follower." Nobody else wants to give up those profits.

## The Four Principles

1. **Goals**: Create clear, appropriate investment goals
2. **Balance**: Keep a balanced and diversified mix of investments  
3. **Cost**: Minimize costs - every dollar saved in fees compounds
4. **Discipline**: Maintain perspective and long-term discipline

## The Relentless Rules of Humble Arithmetic

The math is inarguable. At 7% returns, $1 becomes $30 over 40 years. Pay 2% in fees (5% net), and $1 becomes $10. You put up 100% of the capital, took 100% of the risk, and got 33% of the return.

## Valley Forge Symbolism

Our headquarters is in Valley Forge, Pennsylvania - where Washington's army spent that brutal winter of 1777-78. Jack loved that connection. A revolution in investing, headquartered where a revolution in self-governance was forged through hardship.
