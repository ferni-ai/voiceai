# Vacation Planning Principles

## Start with WHY

Before WHERE, ask:
- What do you want to FEEL on this trip?
- Relaxation? Adventure? Culture? Connection?
- Who's going and what do they need?
- What will make this trip memorable?

The destination should serve the purpose, not the other way around.

## Budget Framework

### Categories
1. **Transportation**: Flights, car rental, local transit
2. **Accommodation**: Hotels, Airbnb, hostels
3. **Food**: Restaurants, groceries, experiences
4. **Activities**: Tours, tickets, experiences
5. **Buffer**: 10-15% for unexpected

### Money-Saving Strategies
- **Shoulder season**: Just before or after peak = same weather, half the crowds
- **Tuesday flights**: Often cheapest day to fly
- **Book 6 weeks ahead**: Sweet spot for domestic flights
- **Apartment rentals**: Kitchen = savings + flexibility
- **Picnic lunches**: Experience local markets, save money

## Timeline Planning

### When to Book
- International flights: 3-6 months ahead
- Domestic flights: 3-6 weeks ahead
- Hotels: Flexible, but popular places book early
- Popular restaurants/tours: Research this - some book months out

### Ideal Trip Length
- Weekend getaway: 2-3 days
- Domestic exploration: 4-7 days
- International discovery: 10-14 days
- Deep immersion: 3+ weeks

## Itinerary Balance

### The 60/40 Rule
- 60% planned activities
- 40% open time

Never schedule every hour. Magic happens in the unplanned moments.

### Daily Rhythm
- One BIG thing per day (museum, hike, tour)
- One SMALL thing (coffee shop, walk, market)
- Built-in rest time (especially with kids or jet lag)

### Avoid
- Over-packing the schedule
- Rushing from thing to thing
- No downtime
- Ignoring travel days (they count!)

## Research Phase

### Questions to Answer
- Entry requirements? (Visas, vaccines, etc.)
- Best neighborhoods to stay?
- Must-do experiences vs. nice-to-haves?
- Local customs to know?
- Safety considerations?

### Sources
- Recent trip reports (blogs, Reddit)
- Official tourism sites
- Guidebooks (still useful!)
- Local experts (tour guides know things)

## Packing Philosophy

### The Capsule Approach
- Neutral base colors
- Everything mixes and matches
- Layers for weather flexibility
- Limit shoes (they're heavy)

### The One-Bag Goal
If possible, carry-on only. Benefits:
- No waiting at baggage claim
- No lost luggage anxiety
- Easier transit/navigation
- Forces thoughtful packing

## Travel Styles

### Solo Travel
- Total freedom, total responsibility
- Safety considerations more important
- Best for: Self-discovery, flexibility lovers

### Couples Travel
- Balance both people's interests
- Build in alone time
- Best for: Deep connection, shared experiences

### Family Travel
- Lower expectations, more flexibility
- Kid-friendly doesn't mean boring
- Best for: Memory-making, bonding

### Group Travel
- Clear budget conversations upfront
- Divide planning responsibilities
- Plan together time AND apart time
- Best for: Shared adventure, friendship building

## Post-Trip

### Don't Skip
- Photo organization (do it while fresh)
- Trip journal or notes
- Thank-you notes to hosts/guides
- Reflection: What worked? What would you change?

The trip isn't really over until you've captured what you want to remember.
