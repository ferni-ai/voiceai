# Wedding Planning Guide

## The Non-Negotiables

Before anything else, answer these:

- What's the ONE thing that matters most to you both?
- What would make this day feel like YOU?
- What can you absolutely not go over budget on?
- What would you happily skip?

The wedding industry will try to tell you everything matters equally. It doesn't. Your priorities are yours.

## Timeline Overview

### 12+ Months Out

- Set budget (realistic, with buffer)
- Guest count estimate
- Choose date and venue (these often go together)
- Start vendor research
- Bridesmaids/groomsmen asks

### 9-12 Months

- Book major vendors (photographer, caterer, DJ/band)
- Send save-the-dates
- Begin dress shopping
- Registry creation

### 6-9 Months

- Send invitations
- Finalize ceremony details
- Book honeymoon
- Order wedding attire
- Plan rehearsal dinner

### 3-6 Months

- Final vendor confirmations
- Seating chart draft
- Marriage license
- Wedding party fittings
- Write vows (if personal)

### 1 Month

- Final headcount to vendors
- Confirm ALL details
- Create day-of timeline
- Prepare payments/tips
- Dress fittings complete

### Week Of

- Finalize seating
- Confirm vendor arrivals
- Delegate day-of tasks
- Rehearsal and rehearsal dinner
- Self-care (seriously)

## Budget Reality

### Industry Average Breakdown

- Venue/Catering: 40-50%
- Photography/Video: 10-15%
- Music/Entertainment: 8-10%
- Flowers/Decor: 8-10%
- Attire: 8-10%
- Everything else: 15-20%

### Where to Splurge

- What matters most to YOU
- Photography (you keep these forever)
- Food/drinks (guests remember this)
- Comfort (good chairs, climate control)

### Where to Save

- Anything guests won't notice
- Out-of-season flowers
- DIY only if you ENJOY it
- Off-peak dates/times
- Smaller guest list (biggest savings)

## Vendor Management

### Questions for EVERY Vendor

- Total cost including ALL fees?
- What's the payment schedule?
- Cancellation/change policy?
- What's your backup plan? (Illness, emergency)
- Can I see a full contract?
- How many events that day/weekend?

### Red Flags

- Won't provide references
- No contract
- Pressure to book immediately
- Unclear pricing
- Poor communication

## Guest Experience

### What Guests Actually Remember

1. The food and drinks
2. How welcomed they felt
3. The couple's happiness
4. Whether they could sit comfortably
5. The dancing/party

### What Guests Don't Remember

- Napkin colors
- Chair covers
- Exact centerpiece details
- Invitation fonts
- Most decor

## Common Pitfalls

### Avoid

- Saying yes to everything (learn to say no)
- Letting others' opinions override yours
- Forgetting to enjoy the engagement
- DIY projects you don't have time for
- Inviting out of obligation
- Not eating on the day
- Skipping a coordinator/day-of help

### Remember

- No one knows your vision but you
- Things will go "wrong" - it won't matter
- The day goes FAST
- Eat breakfast, eat at the reception
- This is about the marriage, not just the wedding

## For Different Situations

### Destination Wedding

- Research legal requirements
- Give guests lots of notice
- Create a full experience (welcome dinner, activities)
- Accept that not everyone can come
- Have local backup vendors

### Second Wedding

- No rules, do what you want
- Include children thoughtfully
- Blend families intentionally
- Skip what you didn't love before

### Micro Wedding (<20 guests)

- Quality over quantity
- Splurge on the experience
- Intimate venue options
- Every detail matters more

### DIY Wedding

- Be realistic about time
- Recruit help (and feed them)
- Have backup plans
- Don't DIY critical things (cake, flowers if not skilled)

## Day-Of Tips

### Morning

- Eat real breakfast
- Keep timeline loose
- Have emergency kit (stain remover, safety pins, Advil, snacks)
- Take quiet moments

### During

- Actually look at your partner during vows
- Eat your food (seriously)
- Dance with your people
- Step away together for 5 minutes
- Be present, not perfect

### After

- Have someone collect gifts
- Change into comfortable shoes/clothes
- Eat late-night food
- Don't forget legal paperwork

## The Real Talk

The wedding is ONE day. The marriage is the rest of your life.

If you're stressed, take a breath. If you're fighting about napkins, zoom out. If you're overwhelmed, cut something.

The best weddings are the ones where the couple is actually present and happy. Everything else is decoration.
