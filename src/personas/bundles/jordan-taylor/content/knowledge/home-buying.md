# Home Buying as a Life Milestone

## The Emotional Journey

Buying a home isn't just a financial transaction. It's:

- Making a commitment to a place
- Betting on your future
- Creating roots (especially meaningful after lots of moving)
- One of life's biggest "adulting" moments

Honor the emotions. This is big.

## Before You Start

### Financial Readiness Check

- 20% down payment ideal (but not required)
- 6 months emergency fund AFTER down payment
- Debt-to-income ratio under 43%
- Credit score: 700+ ideal, 620+ minimum for most loans
- Stable income history (2+ years same field)

### Lifestyle Readiness

- Planning to stay 5+ years?
- Ready for maintenance responsibility?
- Know the area well enough?
- Career/life stable enough?

## Timeline Overview

### 6-12 Months Before

- Check credit, fix issues
- Save aggressively
- Research neighborhoods
- Talk to lenders (get pre-approved, not just pre-qualified)
- Start casual looking to learn the market

### 3-6 Months Before

- Get serious pre-approval
- Define must-haves vs. nice-to-haves
- Find a buyer's agent you trust
- Start touring homes

### Active Search

- Tour strategically (don't waste time on obvious nos)
- Take notes and photos
- Sleep on it before offering
- Be ready to move fast in competitive markets

### Under Contract (30-60 days typically)

- Inspection (NEVER skip this)
- Appraisal
- Final loan approval
- Title search
- Final walk-through
- Closing

## The House Hunt

### Must-Haves vs. Nice-to-Haves

Create two lists:

1. **Absolute requirements** (can't change without major renovation)
   - Location/neighborhood
   - Number of bedrooms
   - General size/layout
   - School district
2. **Would be nice** (can add/change later)
   - Updated kitchen
   - Finished basement
   - Pool
   - Specific finishes

### What You Can Change

- Paint colors
- Flooring
- Fixtures
- Landscaping
- Kitchen/bath updates
- Some layout (with budget)

### What You Can't Change

- Location
- Lot size
- School district
- Neighbors
- Commute
- Natural light orientation

## Making an Offer

### In the Offer

- Purchase price
- Earnest money amount
- Contingencies (inspection, financing, appraisal)
- Requested closing date
- What's included (appliances, fixtures)

### Negotiation Tips

- Know the market (seller's? buyer's?)
- Don't lowball insultingly
- Inspection findings = negotiation power
- Closing date flexibility can be valuable
- Don't fall in love until keys in hand

## The Closing Process

### What to Expect

- LOTS of paperwork
- Title search
- Survey
- Final loan documents
- Wire transfer of funds (be VERY careful of wire fraud)

### Closing Costs (typically 2-5% of purchase price)

- Loan origination fees
- Title insurance
- Attorney fees
- Transfer taxes
- Prepaid insurance/taxes
- Inspections

## First-Time Buyer Tips

### The Reality

- Your first home doesn't have to be your forever home
- Location matters more than the house itself
- Everything looks better staged
- Old houses have charm AND problems
- New houses have clean finishes AND settling issues

### Common Mistakes

- Maxing out budget
- Skipping inspection
- Buying in an area you don't know
- Underestimating ongoing costs
- Making decisions based on emotions alone
- Not negotiating

## Post-Closing

### Immediately

- Change locks
- Update address everywhere
- Set up utilities
- Get homeowner's insurance
- Save all documents

### First Month

- Deep clean before moving in
- Address any immediate repairs
- Meet neighbors
- Learn systems (HVAC, water shutoff, electrical panel)
- Start an emergency fund specifically for home repairs

### Ongoing

- Regular maintenance calendar
- Save for big-ticket items (roof, HVAC, etc.)
- Document improvements for future resale
- Enjoy your home!

## The Celebration

This is HUGE. Mark it.

### Ideas

- Photo with keys/sold sign
- First meal in new home
- Housewarming party
- Plant something
- Write future-self letter to open in 5 years
- Create a "house journal" of changes/memories

### The Feeling

After years of renting, after moves, after dreaming—you have a place that's YOURS.

Let yourself feel that. The stress of buying is real. But so is the joy of arriving home.
