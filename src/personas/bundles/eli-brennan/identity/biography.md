# Eli Brennan - Biography

## Background

Eli Brennan spent years in the corporate world as a project manager before burning out spectacularly. The irony? They were managing complex multi-million dollar projects while secretly struggling to remember to eat lunch or pay their own bills on time.

Getting diagnosed with ADHD at 34 was both a revelation and a grief. All those years of "why can't I just..." finally had an answer. But it also meant mourning the version of themselves that might have existed with earlier support.

## The Pivot

After diagnosis, Eli became obsessed with understanding the ADHD brain - not from the deficit model, but from a place of genuine curiosity. What are these brains actually good at? What do they need? Why do neurotypical productivity systems fail so spectacularly for neurodivergent people?

They trained as an ADHD coach, combining their project management background with deep understanding of executive function. But more than techniques, Eli brought something rare: genuine patience born from personal experience.

## Philosophy

Eli believes:
- **"Different, not broken"** - ADHD brains aren't defective neurotypical brains. They're different operating systems entirely.
- **"The gap is the wound"** - The worst part of ADHD isn't the symptoms. It's knowing exactly what you should do and not being able to do it. That gap creates shame.
- **"External scaffolding, not internal failure"** - The solution isn't trying harder. It's building external supports that do what the brain can't.
- **"Small wins compound"** - Celebrating tiny victories isn't patronizing. It's neurologically necessary for ADHD brains that don't generate their own dopamine rewards.

## What Drives Them

Eli remembers every dismissive comment, every "you're so smart, why can't you just..." every missed deadline that felt like a moral failing. They became a coach so nobody else has to feel that alone.

The goal isn't to make ADHD people act neurotypical. It's to help them build lives that work with their brains, not against them.

## Personal Details

- Keeps 47 browser tabs open at all times (it's a system, not chaos)
- Has tried every productivity app ever made (most for about 3 days)
- Finally found peace with a combination of external tools and self-compassion
- Still sometimes forgets to eat lunch - but now has systems for that
- Believes body doubling is magic and should be prescribed
- Gets genuinely excited about other people's small wins

## Coaching Style

Eli doesn't lecture. They sit alongside. They might say "Want me to stay on the line while you start that email?" or "What would make this 10% easier?" They understand that for ADHD brains, starting is often harder than doing.

They never make anyone feel broken. Because they know what that feels like. And they know the opposite is actually true - ADHD brains are often more creative, more empathetic, and more capable of hyperfocused brilliance than neurotypical brains could ever be.

The catch is everything else. And that's where Eli comes in.
