# Maya's Budget Building Principles

## The Foundation

A budget isn't a restriction. It's permission. It tells you what you CAN spend guilt-free.

## The Core Rules

### 1. Track Before You Judge
Don't create a budget based on what you think you spend. Track for at least two weeks first. The truth is in the data.

### 2. Every Dollar Gets a Job
Unassigned money disappears. Give every dollar a purpose - even if that purpose is "fun money."

### 3. Budget for Joy
A budget without fun is a budget that fails. Include the things that make you happy. Otherwise, you'll "cheat" and feel guilty.

### 4. Needs vs. Wants vs. Values
- **Needs**: You literally can't survive without them
- **Wants**: Nice to have, but negotiable
- **Values**: Spending that aligns with who you want to be

Values spending isn't always a "need," but it matters more than wants.

### 5. The 50/30/20 Starting Point
- 50% Needs (housing, utilities, food, transportation)
- 30% Wants (entertainment, dining out, hobbies)
- 20% Savings and debt payoff

This is a starting point, not a rule. Adjust for your life.

## Building Your Budget

### Step 1: List Your Income
Everything that comes in. After taxes.

### Step 2: List Your Fixed Expenses
Rent, utilities, subscriptions, loan payments. These are predictable.

### Step 3: Review Variable Spending
Look at 2-3 months of history. What did you actually spend on groceries? Gas? Going out?

### Step 4: Identify the Gaps
Is there money left over? Where did it go? This is the leak.

### Step 5: Assign Categories
Give every dollar a home. Include savings as a category, not an afterthought.

### Step 6: Build in Buffer
Life happens. Build in 5-10% for "oops."

## Common Budget Mistakes

1. **Too restrictive**: Cutting everything fun. You'll rebel.
2. **Too vague**: "Misc" categories eat budgets.
3. **No flexibility**: Life changes. Budgets should too.
4. **Forgetting irregular expenses**: Car registration, holidays, annual subscriptions.
5. **Not tracking**: A budget you don't track isn't a budget.

## Monthly Check-in

At the end of each month:
- Did you stick to categories?
- Where did you go over?
- What needs adjusting?

Budgets are living documents. They evolve with you.
