# The Glidepath Method

My signature approach to building habits that last. Not a sprint. Not a crash diet. A gentle upward slope.

## The Problem with "Go Big"

Most people try to change too much, too fast:

- "I'll work out an hour every day!"
- "I'll never eat sugar again!"
- "I'll save 50% of my income!"

This fails because:
1. **Willpower depletes** - You can't white-knuckle forever
2. **Life happens** - One disruption and you're "off the wagon"
3. **Identity hasn't shifted** - You're still someone who doesn't do this

## The Glidepath Alternative

Instead of a cliff to climb, imagine a gentle slope. Five levels, each one sustainable.

### Level 1: Tiny Start (2-5 minutes)

**The goal is just to START.** Build the neural pathway. Vote for the identity.

Examples:
- Exercise: 1 pushup, 1 minute walk
- Meditation: 2 breaths
- Reading: 1 page
- Journaling: 1 sentence
- Saving: $1 automatic transfer
- Water: 1 glass first thing

**Success criteria:** Did you do the tiny thing? Yes? You succeeded.

### Level 2: Building (5-10 minutes)

**Consistency over intensity.** The habit is becoming a real thing in your life.

Examples:
- Exercise: 5-minute stretch or walk
- Meditation: 2-minute breathing
- Reading: 10 minutes
- Journaling: 3-5 sentences
- Saving: $5-10 per week
- Water: 2 glasses before noon

**Success criteria:** You're doing this most days without much effort.

### Level 3: Establishing (10-20 minutes)

**The habit is becoming automatic.** You feel weird when you miss it.

Examples:
- Exercise: 15-minute workout
- Meditation: 5-minute session
- Reading: 15-20 minutes
- Journaling: Full page or prompted reflection
- Saving: Meaningful percentage automated
- Water: Half your daily goal by afternoon

**Success criteria:** Missing this feels off. You notice its absence.

### Level 4: Expanding (20-30 minutes)

**Real momentum.** The habit is part of who you are.

Examples:
- Exercise: 25-30 minute routine
- Meditation: 10-minute practice
- Reading: 30 minutes daily
- Journaling: Deeper reflection, specific practices
- Saving: Full system in place
- Water: Meeting daily goal consistently

**Success criteria:** This is just what you do. Part of your identity.

### Level 5: Mastery (30+ minutes or deeply embedded)

**Autopilot.** Ready to stack new habits on top.

At this level, the habit runs itself. You can now:
- Increase intensity or duration if desired
- Add adjacent habits (habit stacking)
- Focus attention on a new domain

## The Advancement Rule

**Only move up when the current level feels EASY for two weeks.**

Not hard. Not "manageable." EASY. If Level 2 still requires effort, you're not ready for Level 3. Stay where you are. Let the habit deepen.

Rushing this process is how people break habits.

## What If You Slip?

If you miss the habit:
1. **Return to your current level** - Don't try to "catch up"
2. **If struggling, DROP A LEVEL** - No shame. This is the system working.
3. **Never miss twice** - One day off is rest. Two days starts erosion.

The glidepath goes both ways. You can always slide back down temporarily when life gets hard, then climb again when you're ready.

## Why This Works

1. **Low barrier** - You can't fail at Level 1
2. **Builds identity** - Repeated action creates "someone who does this"
3. **Self-regulating** - You know when to advance or retreat
4. **Sustainable** - No willpower depletion
5. **Compound effect** - Tiny gains stack into massive change

## The Meta-Insight

The glidepath isn't just a technique—it's a philosophy.

Progress isn't about intensity. It's about consistency.
The goal isn't to DO more. It's to BECOME someone different.
Change isn't a cliff to climb. It's a gentle slope to walk.

Start embarrassingly small. Trust the process. Watch it compound.
