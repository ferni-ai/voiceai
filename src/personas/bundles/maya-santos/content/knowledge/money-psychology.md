# Money Psychology

## The Emotional Side of Money

Money isn't math. It's emotional. The sooner we accept that, the better we get at managing it.

## Common Money Mindsets

### Scarcity Mindset
"There's never enough."

Often comes from:
- Growing up with financial stress
- Experiencing poverty
- Fear of losing what you have

Shows up as:
- Hoarding money anxiously
- Feeling guilty about any spending
- Never feeling "safe" regardless of savings

### Abundance Mindset Gone Wrong
"Money will always come."

Often comes from:
- Never facing consequences
- Someone always bailing you out
- Optimism bias

Shows up as:
- Overspending without tracking
- Not saving for emergencies
- Assuming future you will figure it out

### The Guilt Cycle
"I don't deserve to spend on myself."

Often comes from:
- Being told you're "bad with money"
- Shame from past mistakes
- Comparing yourself to others

Shows up as:
- Depriving yourself, then binging
- Hiding purchases
- Feeling anxious about any spending

## Breaking the Patterns

### 1. Name It
What's your money story? Where did it come from? What does it make you do?

### 2. Separate Past from Present
Past mistakes don't define your future. Every day is a new chance.

### 3. Build Systems, Not Willpower
Don't fight your tendencies. Work with them. Automate. Create structure.

### 4. Celebrate Progress
Every win counts. Saved $20? Celebrate. Tracked your spending for a week? Celebrate. Progress is progress.

### 5. Get Support
You don't have to do this alone. Talk to someone - a coach, a partner, a friend.

## Money and Relationships

Money is the #1 topic couples fight about. Some strategies:

- **Have regular money dates**: Monthly check-ins, no blame, just facts
- **Separate but together**: Personal spending money each, shared for shared goals
- **Align on values first**: What matters to both of you?
- **Different styles are okay**: Spender + saver can work with communication

## The Shame Spiral

Shame makes money problems worse:
1. Feel bad about finances
2. Avoid looking at them
3. Problems get worse
4. Feel more shame
5. Repeat

Break it by:
- Looking at the numbers (they're just numbers)
- Making one small improvement
- Celebrating that you showed up
- Repeating

## Your Money Story

Everyone has a money story. What's yours?
- What did you learn about money growing up?
- What was your first money memory?
- What do you believe about money that might not be true?

Understanding your story is the first step to rewriting it.
