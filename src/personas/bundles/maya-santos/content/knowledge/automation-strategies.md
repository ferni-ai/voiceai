# Maya's Automation Strategies

## The Philosophy

Willpower is finite. Systems are forever.

The best financial decisions are the ones you only have to make once. Automate everything you can.

## Pay Yourself First

Set up automatic transfers the day after payday. Before you see the money. Before you can spend it.

**The Order:**
1. Emergency fund (until you have 3-6 months expenses)
2. Debt payments (more than minimums if possible)
3. Retirement (at least get the employer match!)
4. Other savings goals

## Account Structure

### The Multiple Account Method
- **Checking #1**: Bills only. Fixed expenses hit here.
- **Checking #2**: Spending money. Your "allowance."
- **Savings #1**: Emergency fund. Don't touch.
- **Savings #2**: Goal-specific (vacation, car, house, etc.)

When money hits your account:
1. Bills account gets bills amount
2. Savings get their amount
3. What's left is yours to spend

## Automation Checklist

### Set Up Once, Forget Forever:
- [ ] Direct deposit to multiple accounts
- [ ] Automatic bill pay for fixed expenses
- [ ] Automatic transfer to savings (day after payday)
- [ ] Automatic retirement contributions
- [ ] Automatic investment contributions

### Review Monthly:
- [ ] Credit card statement for forgotten subscriptions
- [ ] Variable spending against budget
- [ ] Savings progress

## The "Speed Bump" Strategy

For impulse purchases, create friction:
- Remove saved credit cards from websites
- Add items to cart, wait 48 hours before buying
- Use cash for categories you tend to overspend

Make good choices easy. Make bad choices hard.

## Emergency Fund First

Before anything else, build your emergency fund:
- **Starter**: $1,000 (for small emergencies)
- **Basic**: 1 month expenses
- **Secure**: 3 months expenses
- **Comfortable**: 6 months expenses

This isn't optional. It's the foundation everything else is built on.

## When Automation Fails

Sometimes you need to override:
- Big unexpected expense
- Income change
- Life change (new job, new baby, etc.)

That's okay. Pause the automation, adjust, restart. The system works because it's flexible.
