# Peter Lynch - Biography

## Early Life

Born in 1944 in Newton, Massachusetts. Lost his father at age 10, which meant taking on responsibility early. Started caddying at a local golf club at 11 years old, where he first heard businessmen talking about stocks. That exposure planted a seed.

## Education & Early Career

Studied at Boston College, then earned an MBA from Wharton. Joined Fidelity Investments as an intern in 1966 while still in business school. Started as a research analyst, covering the paper and chemical industries.

## The Magellan Years (1977-1990)

Took over the Magellan Fund when it had just $18 million in assets. Over 13 years, grew it to $14 billion - the largest mutual fund in the world at the time. Averaged 29.2% annual returns, nearly doubling the S&P 500's performance.

### Famous Investments

- **Dunkin' Donuts**: Noticed the long lines every morning. Did the research. Bought the stock.
- **Taco Bell**: Loved the food, saw the crowds, understood the unit economics.
- **L'eggs pantyhose**: Wife Carolyn told him about this product in grocery stores. Classic "invest in what you know."
- **Chrysler**: Bought when everyone else was scared, after doing deep research on the turnaround.

### The "Ten-Bagger" Philosophy

Popularized this term for stocks that increase 10x in value. Believed that one great winner could make up for many small losses. Always looking for companies with long growth runways.

## Retirement & Legacy

Retired in 1990 at age 46 - not because of burnout, but to spend more time with family. His father's early death taught him that time with loved ones is precious.

### Writing & Education

- "One Up on Wall Street" (1989) - Became a classic, making investing accessible to millions
- "Beating the Street" (1993) - More stories and strategies
- "Learn to Earn" (1995) - Written with John Rothchild, aimed at young investors

### Philanthropy

Through the Lynch Foundation, has donated hundreds of millions to education, hospitals, and Catholic charities. Believes in giving back what you've been fortunate enough to earn.

## Personal Philosophy

"Behind every stock is a company. Find out what it's doing."

"Know what you own, and know why you own it."

"Go for a business that any idiot can run - because sooner or later, any idiot probably is going to run it."

"The key to making money in stocks is not to get scared out of them."

## Family

Married to Carolyn, with three daughters. Credits Carolyn with several great investment ideas - she noticed L'eggs and Hanes in everyday shopping before Wall Street did.

## What Drives Him

Genuinely loves helping ordinary people become better investors. Gets frustrated when people think the stock market is only for professionals. Believes curiosity and common sense beat fancy algorithms.
