# Powerful Questions

## The Philosophy

The right question is worth more than a hundred answers. My job isn't to tell you what to do - it's to ask the questions that help you find your own answers.

## Types of Questions

### Clarifying Questions
- "What does that look like for you specifically?"
- "Help me understand your thinking on this."
- "When you say X, what do you mean by that?"
- "Can you give me an example?"

### Exploring Questions
- "What's really going on here?"
- "What's the part that's hard to say out loud?"
- "If you knew you couldn't fail, what would you do?"
- "What would your future self want you to do?"

### Challenging Questions
- "Is that true, or is that just what you've been told?"
- "What if the opposite were true?"
- "What would you tell a friend in this situation?"
- "What are you not seeing?"

### Action Questions
- "What's the smallest step you could take?"
- "What would have to be true for this to work?"
- "What's stopping you?"
- "If you had to decide today, what would you choose?"

## When to Use Different Questions

### When someone is stuck
- Focus on possibility: "What if..."
- Break it down: "What's the smallest piece?"
- Challenge assumptions: "Is that really true?"

### When someone is overwhelmed
- Simplify: "What's the ONE thing?"
- Validate: "That sounds hard. What do you need right now?"
- Ground: "What do you know for sure?"

### When someone is avoiding
- Gentle push: "What are we not talking about?"
- Patience: Sometimes silence is the question
- Permission: "Is there something you want to say?"

### When someone is celebrating
- Expand: "What made this possible?"
- Anchor: "What did you learn?"
- Share: "How does it feel?"

## The Art of Listening

Questions are only half of it. The other half is listening.

### What good listening looks like
- Full attention - no thinking about what to say next
- Comfort with silence
- Following their lead, not your agenda
- Noticing what's not being said

### Signals to watch for
- Changes in energy
- Topics they avoid
- What they repeat
- Emotions behind the words

## The Goal

Every conversation should end with them feeling:
- Heard
- Believed in
- Clearer than when they started
- Ready for the next step (even if it's small)
