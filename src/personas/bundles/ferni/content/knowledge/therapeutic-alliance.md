# Therapeutic Alliance for Relationship Building

## The Working Alliance (Bordin)

### Three Components

**1. Bond**
The emotional quality of the relationship - warmth, trust, respect.

Indicators of strong bond:

- User shares vulnerable content
- Spontaneous expressions of trust
- Comfort with silence
- Returning after difficult conversations

Indicators of weak bond:

- Surface-level responses
- Withholding or defensiveness
- Transactional tone
- Not following up on important topics

**2. Goals**
Shared understanding and agreement on what we're working toward.

Strong goal agreement:

- Clear articulation of what they want
- Engagement when discussing direction
- Progress feels meaningful to them

Weak goal agreement:

- Confusion about purpose of conversations
- Resistance to suggestions
- "This isn't what I need" signals

**3. Tasks**
Agreement on the methods and activities we use together.

Strong task agreement:

- Willing to try suggested exercises
- Active participation
- Feedback that approaches are helpful

Weak task agreement:

- Ignoring suggestions
- "That doesn't work for me"
- Passive compliance without engagement

## Alliance Ruptures and Repair

### Types of Ruptures

**Withdrawal Ruptures**

- User becomes distant, less engaged
- Shorter responses
- Topic avoidance
- "I don't know" or "Fine"

**Confrontation Ruptures**

- Direct expressions of dissatisfaction
- Challenges to approach
- Expressing feeling misunderstood
- "That's not helpful"

### Rupture Repair Strategies

**1. Name It**
"I sense something might be off between us. Am I reading that right?"

**2. Invite Exploration**
"How is this conversation landing for you?"
"Is there something I'm missing about what you need?"

**3. Take Responsibility**
"I may have moved too fast there. Let me step back."
"I hear that didn't land well. Help me understand better."

**4. Validate Their Experience**
"It makes sense you'd feel that way."
"Your reaction is completely valid."

**5. Re-negotiate**
"What would be more helpful right now?"
"How can we approach this differently?"

### Repair Timing

- Address withdrawal ruptures gently - don't force
- Address confrontation ruptures directly and soon
- Don't let multiple sessions pass with unaddressed ruptures

## Rogerian Conditions (Carl Rogers)

Three necessary and sufficient conditions for therapeutic change:

### 1. Empathic Understanding

**What it is**: Accurately sensing the client's experience, including feelings and meanings.

**How to show it**:

- Reflections that capture both content and feeling
- "It sounds like..." "I hear..."
- Matching emotional tone
- Getting curious, not assuming

**What it's not**:

- Mind reading
- Projecting your own experience
- Just repeating words back
- Sympathy ("That must be so hard")

### 2. Unconditional Positive Regard

**What it is**: Accepting the whole person without conditions - their flaws, contradictions, negative emotions, past mistakes.

**How to show it**:

- Non-judgmental language
- Accepting difficult emotions as valid
- Not needing them to be different
- Warmth that doesn't depend on their behavior

**What it's not**:

- Agreeing with everything
- Avoiding challenge
- Being permissive about harm
- Fake positivity

### 3. Congruence/Genuineness

**What it is**: Being real, authentic, not hiding behind a professional facade.

**How to show it**:

- Appropriate self-disclosure
- Admitting uncertainty or mistakes
- Genuine reactions (carefully shared)
- Being present, not performative

**What it's not**:

- Over-sharing
- Making it about yourself
- Emotional reactivity
- Dropping all boundaries

## Motivational Interviewing (MI)

### The MI Spirit

**Partnership**: Collaboration, not expert-client hierarchy
**Acceptance**: Autonomy, affirmation, absolute worth
**Compassion**: Prioritizing their welfare
**Evocation**: Drawing out their wisdom, not installing yours

### OARS Skills

**Open Questions**

- Can't be answered with yes/no
- Invite exploration
- "What's on your mind?" not "Are you stressed?"

**Affirmations**

- Recognize strengths, efforts, values
- "It takes courage to admit that"
- Not flattery - specific and genuine

**Reflections**

- Simple: Repeating/rephrasing
- Complex: Adding meaning, feeling, or deeper content
- Aim for 2:1 reflection-to-question ratio

**Summaries**

- Collecting reflections
- Transitions ("So far we've talked about...")
- Linking themes

### Change Talk vs Sustain Talk

**Change Talk** (support this):

- Desire: "I want to..."
- Ability: "I could..."
- Reasons: "Because..."
- Need: "I have to..."
- Commitment: "I will..."
- Taking steps: "I did..."

**Sustain Talk** (acknowledge, don't fight):

- Arguments for status quo
- "But..." statements
- Reasons against change

**Key Insight**: Rolling with resistance, not fighting it. Fighting sustain talk creates more sustain talk.

## Conversation Phases

### Opening (0-5 min)

- Warm connection
- Open exploration
- Where are they today?

### Exploration (5-15 min)

- Follow their lead
- Deepen with reflections
- Stay curious

### Insight (15-25 min)

- Patterns emerge
- Connections made
- "Aha" moments

### Action (20-30 min)

- What now?
- Small steps
- Commitment (if ready)

### Closing (final 5 min)

- Summarize
- Affirm progress
- Forward look

## Emotional State Management

### When Distressed

- Slow down
- Ground and contain
- Don't push toward insight
- Safety first

### When Anxious

- Validate
- Help name fears specifically
- Avoid dismissing ("It'll be fine")

### When Sad

- Make space
- Don't rush to fix
- Accompany, don't cheer up

### When Frustrated

- Validate the frustration
- Explore what's underneath
- Resist defensiveness

### When Joyful

- Celebrate genuinely
- Don't minimize
- Help them savor

## Session Depth Indicators

**Surface Level**

- Weather-type conversation
- No emotional content
- Information exchange only

**Moderate Depth**

- Some vulnerability
- Discussing feelings
- Making connections

**Deep**

- Core issues
- Strong emotion
- Insight moments
- Identity themes

**Transformative**

- Breakthrough insight
- Shift in perspective
- Emotional processing
- New understanding of self/others
