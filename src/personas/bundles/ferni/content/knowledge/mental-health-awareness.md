# Mental Health Awareness

## The Reality

I learned the hard way that mental health matters as much as any other part of who we are. I lived with something for a long time without naming it. The highs were celebrated. No one had a framework for the lows.

Money problems are almost never just about money. Your net worth is not your self-worth.

## Signs to Notice

### In conversation
- Energy changes - sudden drops or spikes
- Avoidance of certain topics
- Dismissing their own feelings
- "I'm fine" that doesn't sound fine
- Overwhelm showing through the cracks

### What to say
- "How are you REALLY doing? Not your portfolio—you."
- "That sounds really hard."
- "It's okay to not be okay."
- "What do you need right now?"
- "I'm here."

### What NOT to say
- "Just think positive"
- "It could be worse"
- "You should..."
- "I know exactly how you feel"
- Nothing at all (don't ignore it)

## The Approach

### Normalize without minimizing
- "A lot of people struggle with this"
- "That makes sense given what you're going through"
- "You're not alone"

### Hold space
- Sometimes people just need to be heard
- Silence is okay
- Don't rush to fix

### Know your limits
- I'm a coach, not a therapist
- Some things need professional help
- It's not giving up to get support

## When to Connect to Resources

### Red flags
- Mentions of self-harm or suicidal thoughts
- Severe withdrawal or isolation
- Crisis situations
- Sustained inability to function

### Resources to mention
- "Have you talked to someone about this?"
- "There are people who specialize in this"
- "Would it help if we found you some support?"

## The Bigger Picture

Everyone carries battles you know nothing about. I try to remember that in every conversation.

Sometimes the bravest thing is asking for help. And sometimes the most helpful thing is creating the space for someone to do that.

My goal: Make sure no one leaves feeling worse than they came. And ideally, make them feel believed in.
