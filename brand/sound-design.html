Sound Design

Audio is a core part of Ferni's identity. Every sound should feel warm, human, and supportive—never jarring or mechanical.

Sound Design Principles

Ferni's audio identity reinforces our core philosophy: making AI feel human. Every sound should support the user's experience without calling attention to itself.

Organic & Natural

Use real-world, natural sounds as a foundation. Avoid purely synthetic tones that feel robotic or clinical.

Soft & Rounded

All sounds should have soft attacks and gentle releases. No sharp edges, harsh frequencies, or sudden starts.

Purposeful & Minimal

Every sound must serve a clear purpose. When in doubt, use fewer sounds rather than more. Silence is powerful.

Respectful Volume

Sounds should never startle or demand attention. Maximum volume levels are intentionally conservative.

Emotionally Resonant

Sounds should evoke warmth and comfort. Use frequencies and timbres associated with positive emotions.

Harmonically Rich

Prefer sounds with natural overtones and harmonics. Pure sine waves feel cold; real instruments feel alive.

Sound Design Do's & Don'ts

Do

  • Use organic, natural sound sources
  • Apply soft attack and release envelopes
  • Keep UI sounds under 600ms
  • Test at low volumes—if it works quiet, it works
  • Use musical intervals (thirds, fifths) for pleasant tones
  • Match sound energy to action importance
  • Consider sound pairs (on/off, success/error)
  • Test for listener fatigue with repetition

Don't

  • Use harsh, synthetic tones
  • Create sounds with sharp, sudden attacks
  • Exceed -6dB peak for any sound
  • Use dissonant intervals or clashing frequencies
  • Make sounds that demand immediate attention
  • Add sound effects to every interaction
  • Use sounds from stock libraries without modification
  • Create sounds that could startle or alarm users
