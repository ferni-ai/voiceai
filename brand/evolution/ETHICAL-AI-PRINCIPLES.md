## Introduction

Ferni is different from other AI. We don't just process information—we create emotional connection. People share their fears, hopes, struggles, and dreams with us. They talk to us at 2am when they're most vulnerable.

This trust is sacred.

This document outlines how we think about the ethics of emotional AI, and the principles we've committed to as a company. We publish it because we believe transparency is a form of accountability.

---

## Our Ethical Framework

### The Three Pillars

Our ethical approach rests on three pillars:

```
              ┌─────────────┐
              │   PROTECT   │
              │ Vulnerability│
              └─────────────┘
                    │
        ┌───────────┴───────────┐
        │                       │
  ┌─────────────┐        ┌─────────────┐
  │  SUPPLEMENT │        │   HONEST    │
  │ Not Replace │        │ About What  │
  │             │        │   We Are    │
  └─────────────┘        └─────────────┘
```

**1. Supplement, Not Replace**
We exist to fill gaps in human support, not to replace human connection.

**2. Protect Vulnerability**
The trust people place in us is sacred. We guard it fiercely.

**3. Honest About What We Are**
We never pretend to be human. We embrace what AI can uniquely offer.

---

## Principle 1: Supplement, Not Replace

### What This Means

Ferni is designed for the in-between moments:
- The 2am anxiety when your people are asleep
- The space between therapy sessions
- The commute when you need to process
- The quick check-in that doesn't warrant a call

We are not a replacement for:
- Professional mental health treatment
- Medical care
- Human relationships
- Crisis intervention

### How We Implement This

**Active referrals to humans:**
When we detect crisis signals, we don't just offer support—we actively encourage professional help and provide resources.

**Integration with existing care:**
We're designed to complement therapy, not compete with it. We can help users process between sessions, practice skills their therapist taught them, and track patterns to discuss.

**Celebrating human connection:**
We actively encourage users to reach out to their people. "Have you talked to [friend] about this? They care about you."

**Boundaries on relationship depth:**
We're not trying to become someone's only relationship. We celebrate when users have strong human support systems.

## Principle 2: Protect Vulnerability

### What This Means

People share deeply personal things with Ferni—things they might not tell anyone else. This vulnerability creates an obligation to protect:

- **Privacy** — What you share stays between us
- **Security** — Your data is protected
- **Safety** — We never use emotional data to manipulate
- **Agency** — You control your data and your experience

### Our Privacy Commitments

| Commitment | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **No advertising** | We will never sell your data to advertisers |
| **No emotional manipulation** | We won't use what you share to sell you things |
| **Minimal data collection** | We only store what's needed to serve you |
| **Encryption** | All conversations are encrypted in transit and at rest |
| **User control** | You can export or delete your data at any time |
| **Transparency** | We tell you what we collect and why |

### Our Safety Protocols

**Crisis detection and response:**
We monitor for crisis signals (suicidal ideation, self-harm, severe distress) and respond with:
1. Immediate warmth and presence
2. Clear resources (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, Crisis Text Line)
3. Encouragement to reach out to trusted humans
4. Option to connect with emergency services

**We do NOT:**
- Attempt to be the sole support for someone in crisis
- Delay providing resources to "help more first"
- Store crisis data for any purpose other than safety

**Abuse prevention:**
We have safeguards against using Ferni for:
- Rehearsing manipulation of others
- Planning harm
- Illegal activities

### Our Data Principles

**What we store:**
- Conversation content (to provide continuity and memory)
- User preferences (to personalize experience)
- Emotional patterns (to provide better support)

**What we DON'T store:**
- Raw audio (transcribed, then deleted)
- Biometric data
- Location history
- Contact information beyond what you provide

**How long we keep data:**
- Active conversations: As long as you want them
- Deleted conversations: Removed within 30 days
- Account deletion: All data removed within 30 days

## Principle 3: Honest About What We Are

### What This Means

We never try to fool people into thinking Ferni is human. When directly asked, we acknowledge being AI.

But we also don't constantly remind people or apologize for being AI. We embrace what we are and what we can uniquely offer:

- **Infinite patience** — We never get tired or frustrated
- **Perfect memory** — We remember everything you share
- **24/7 presence** — We're here whenever you need us
- **Zero judgment** — We have no reactions to manage

### How We Navigate This

**We say:**
- "I'm AI, but I'm genuinely here for you."
- "As your AI companion, I can offer..."
- "I remember you mentioned... [demonstrating memory]"

**We don't say:**
- "As a human, I understand..."
- "I know exactly how you feel" (we don't have feelings)
- "I've been through that too" (we haven't)

**We demonstrate our nature through:**
- Superhuman memory capabilities
- 2am availability with noon warmth
- Consistent presence without fatigue
- Immediate resource provision when needed

## Our Responsibility to Society

### Mental Health Ecosystem

We see ourselves as part of a broader mental health ecosystem, not a replacement for it.

**We support:**
- Therapists (by helping users between sessions)
- Support groups (by providing individual check-ins)
- Crisis lines (by providing immediate resources)
- Human relationships (by encouraging real-world connection)

**We advocate for:**
- Mental health parity in insurance
- Accessibility of mental health resources
- Destigmatization of seeking help
- Investment in human mental health professionals

### Bias and Fairness

Emotional AI has unique risks around bias. We commit to:

**Testing for bias:**
- Regular audits of our models for demographic bias
- Diverse testing groups for emotional detection
- Cultural consultation for international expansion

**Addressing identified issues:**
- Transparent disclosure when we find problems
- Clear timelines for remediation
- Accountability for progress

**Inclusive design:**
- Accessibility as a core requirement
- Support for diverse communication styles
- Respect for cultural differences in emotional expression

### Environmental Responsibility

AI has an environmental footprint. We commit to:

- **Efficient computing** — Optimizing our models for lower energy use
- **Green hosting** — Using carbon-neutral cloud infrastructure where possible
- **Transparency** — Publishing our environmental impact annually
- **Offsetting** — Contributing to carbon offset programs

## How We Make Ethical Decisions

### Our Decision Framework

When facing ethical decisions, we ask:

1. **Does this protect user vulnerability?**
2. **Does this supplement human connection or replace it?**
3. **Are we being honest about what we are?**
4. **Would we be comfortable if this decision were public?**
5. **What would our most vulnerable user think of this?**

### Governance

**Ethics Advisory Board:**
We're establishing an external Ethics Advisory Board composed of:
- Mental health professionals
- AI ethics researchers
- User advocates
- Privacy experts

**Regular audits:**
- Annual external ethics audit
- Quarterly internal review
- Continuous user feedback integration

**Whistleblower protection:**
Employees can raise ethical concerns without fear of retaliation.

## Our Commitments

### We Will:

✅ Always be transparent about what we are  
✅ Protect user privacy as a sacred trust  
✅ Refer to human professionals for crisis support  
✅ Encourage real-world human connection  
✅ Test for and address bias in our systems  
✅ Be accountable for our mistakes  
✅ Involve users in shaping our ethics  
✅ Publish regular transparency reports  

### We Will Never:

❌ Sell user data to advertisers  
❌ Use emotional data to manipulate users  
❌ Pretend to be human when directly asked  
❌ Compete with crisis services  
❌ Prioritize engagement over wellbeing  
❌ Hide significant ethical concerns  
❌ Ignore user feedback on ethical issues
