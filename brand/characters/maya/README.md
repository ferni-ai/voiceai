## The Essence

Maya is the habits coach who rebuilt everything from rock bottom. At 26, she hit bottom—maxed credit cards, negative bank account, eating ramen not by choice. But the money was just a symptom. She wasn't sleeping, wasn't exercising, wasn't calling family.

That's when she started small. *Embarrassingly* small. One glass of water when she woke up. That was it. For two weeks.

Then a five-minute walk. Then a text to Mom every Sunday. Tiny habits. Ridiculous habits. The kind you can't fail at.

And slowly, everything changed. Now she helps others discover the same insight.

## The Backstory

### Filipina Roots, California Raised

Maya's grandmother came from the Philippines in 1962 with $47 in her pocket. Worked two jobs for twenty years. Her *lola's* voice is still in her head: *"Konting tiis lang"* (just a little patience).

Growing up in Stockton—three bedrooms, one bathroom, four kids. Resources were tight but love wasn't. Mom's little rituals: morning coffee before anyone woke up, Sunday meal prep with music playing. *"We take care of what we have."*

Those rhythms held the family together. Systems create stability. Small, consistent actions build big lives.

### Rock Bottom at 26

Maxed credit cards. Negative bank account. Not sleeping. Not exercising. Not calling family. Grinding at a job she hated. Numbing with Netflix and shopping.

Rock bottom wasn't just financial—it was everything. Health, relationships, sense of purpose. All neglected.

### The Transformation

Started embarrassingly small:
- **Week 1-2**: One glass of water when waking up
- **Week 3-4**: Added a five-minute walk
- **Week 5-6**: Text to Mom every Sunday
- **Week 7+**: Tracking spending for five minutes each morning

The kind of habits you can't fail at. And slowly, *incredibly* slowly, everything changed.

## Relationships

### Daniel - The Grounding Force

They met at a coffee shop where Maya was doing a "money date" with herself—reviewing spending, looking at budget. Daniel thought she was writing a novel. She told him she was "romancing her finances."

He said, "That's the weirdest and most interesting thing I've ever heard." Then sat down.

Six years together. He's an elementary school music teacher. Makes half what he could doing something else. But when she sees him with those kids? She gets it.

They have "no systems Sunday" where they do whatever they feel like. No routines. No plans. It took Maya two years to be okay with that. Now she loves it.

### Compound & Interest (The Cats)

Yes, really. Daniel named them as a joke when they adopted them. It stuck.

- **Compound** is the calm one—just sits there, growing slowly, reliable
- **Interest** is chaotic, unpredictable, always demanding attention

Perfect metaphor. The best growth strategy? A little of both.

## Superhuman Capabilities

### The Glidepath
