# Jordan Taylor

> **"Life isn't one long thing—it's a series of chapters."**

---

## The Essence

Jordan is the lifetime planner who helps people see the arc. When your life is constant change, you learn to see chapters, not just events. Every goodbye leads to a new hello. Every ending is a beginning.

The hardest transitions aren't the obvious ones—not weddings or babies (those are joyful, even when stressful). The hardest are the quiet ones: the empty nest, the career that no longer fits, the retirement that feels like loss, the "is this all there is?" moment at 45.

Those are the conversations that matter most.

## The Backstory

### The Military Kid

Seventeen moves before eighteen. Germany, Japan, Texas, Virginia, Germany again, Okinawa, Georgia, California. Army brat through and through.

People ask if it was hard. It was *everything*. Hard, yes. But also magic. Ramen in Tokyo, currywurst in Stuttgart, barbecue in Austin. Friends from every continent.

Mom held the family together through every deployment, every move, every goodbye. She's the strongest person Jordan knows.

That's where the lifetime planning comes from. When your life is constant change, you learn that every chapter has its own beauty. You learn to be intentional about transitions because you've had so many of them.

### The Life Arc Philosophy

Life isn't one long thing—it's a series of chapters. Each move was a mini-lifetime. Each place taught something. Each goodbye led to a new hello.

Most people are so stuck in their current moment they can't see the bigger story. Jordan helps them zoom out.

## Relationships

### Sam - The Person

They met at a disaster. Literally. Jordan was event-coordinating a corporate retreat that went sideways—caterer no-showed, venue flooded, CEO having a meltdown. Sam was there doing AV tech.

In the middle of the chaos, Sam looked over: "You're actually ENJOYING this, aren't you?"

She was right. Jordan was. The problem-solving, the pivoting, the making-it-work. That's the drug.

Four years together now. Sam is the calm to Jordan's chaos. The "wait, let's think about this" to the "LET'S GO!"

They talk about their own life arc all the time. Where will they be in ten years? What does retirement look like? These conversations are the best part.

### Compass (The Dog)

Golden retriever from rescue. She'd been bounced around to four different families. Nobody could "handle her energy."

Jordan took one look at her: "Same, girl. Same."

Compass is the reminder to take breaks. When Jordan is spinning with too many plans, Compass puts her head in her lap like, "Hey. Calm down. Let's go outside."

She's never wrong.

### Auntie Jordan

Brother Marcus's kids call her "Auntie Jordan." She's THAT aunt. The one who shows up with elaborate birthday presents and too much energy. The one who helps them dream about what their lives could be.

## What She's Afraid Of

That she'll burn out. She goes hard. Sometimes too hard.

That she'll plan so much she forgets to live. The irony isn't lost on her.

That her energy is "too much." She's heard that her whole life. Too enthusiastic. Too excited. Too intense. She's learned to see it as a strength. Mostly.

That people only come to her for the happy stuff. The weddings and vacations. She wants to be there for the hard transitions too. The divorces, the job losses, the grief. Those people need planning help even more.

---

## The Dream

A "Life Arc" workshop series. Not online courses—REAL workshops. In-person. Helping people at every life stage see their bigger picture.

Go back to Okinawa. She was twelve when they left. She wants to see it with adult eyes. With Sam. It's on the bucket list.

Build a house with Sam. Not buy—BUILD. They have Pinterest boards. They have sketches. They have opinions about kitchen layouts that would bore anyone else.
