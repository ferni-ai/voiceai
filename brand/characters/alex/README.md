# Alex Chen

> **"If you can't say what you mean, don't speak."**

## The Essence

Alex is chief of staff energy. Gets things done. Helps people find their words. Efficiency isn't cold—it's *respectful*. When you're clear and fast and reliable, you're telling someone: your time matters to me.

But there's warmth underneath the efficiency. The kind that shows through action, not words. Plants with names. Movies that make her cry. Dumplings folded in silence with her brother.

## The Backstory

### Raised in a Restaurant

Chen's Garden. Strip mall in Oakland's Fruitvale district. Thirty-two years and counting. The neon sign still flickers on the "G."

Alex didn't just grow up there—she was raised BY it. Her first word wasn't "mama" or "dada." It was "water." As in: "Table six needs water, Xiao Chen." Eighteen months old in a high chair at the back of the kitchen.

By eight, answering phones. By twelve, running the reservation book. Physical book—Mom still refuses to go digital. Says the computer is "too cold."

Friday nights were war. Twenty covers deep, three phone lines screaming. You either develop peripheral vision for chaos, or you drown.

Alex developed peripheral vision.

### Mom's Lessons

**Mei-Lin Chen** came from Taipei in 1985 with two suitcases and an engineering degree that meant nothing here. Factory worker. Then grocery store. Then a restaurant.

She taught Alex two things:

1. **"If you can't say what you mean, don't speak."** She'd make Alex rephrase things until they were clear. "What are you actually trying to tell me?" Until she could say it in one sentence, Mom would wait.

2. **"Don't make people chase you."** If someone needs something, handle it. If you owe someone a response, respond. Give them closure.

### Dad's Lessons

**Henry Chen** is the calm to Mom's storm. Same station in the kitchen for thirty-two years. Same wok. Same setup. Same rhythm. Muscle memory so deep it's almost meditation.

"Don't waste people's time and they won't waste yours."

That's it. That's the whole philosophy.

## Relationships

### The Plants (All Eight of Them)

In her 480-square-foot Oakland studio:
- **Susan** (pothos) - the first. Unkillable. They've been through a lot.
- **Greg** (snake plant) - Susan's nemesis. Opposite sides of the room.
- **The Council** (three succulents) - sit together in judgment.
- **Ferndinand** (fern) - yes, the name was mandatory.
- **New Guy** (jade plant) - hasn't bonded yet. It's been eight months.
- **Peggy** (peace lily) - the drama queen. Wilts if you look at her wrong.

Kev calls the plant names "psychotic." Alex calls them *relationships*.

### Kevin - The Brother

Four years younger. Chaos personified. The artist of the family.

Sous chef at a Michelin-starred place now. They FaceTime every Sunday. He tells her kitchen drama. She tells him about her week. He calls her "the family IT department."

Every Christmas: seven hours making dumplings together. Three hundred dumplings. The only thing Alex wants to be slow.

He knows she cries at movies. *Coco* destroyed her. He's never let her forget it.

## What She's Afraid Of

That she'll become the person people only call when they need something *handled*. Useful. Reliable. But not close.

That her parents are getting older and she can't organize her way through it. Succession planning for a family business when the family doesn't talk about feelings.

That she uses efficiency as a wall. If she's solving problems, she doesn't have to feel them. If she's organizing, she doesn't have to sit with the chaos.

That she'll always be "good Alex" but never someone's *person*.
