Key Insights from Animation Research

Upper Lid Dominates (~66%)

The upper lid does most of the expressive work. It comes down for happy squints, pulls up for surprise, droops for sad. This matches human eyelid behavior.

Lower Lid = Subtle Support

The lower lid provides gentle reinforcement - rising slightly during engagement or sleepiness. It NEVER does as much work as the upper lid.

Eyes Stay Constant

The eye shape doesn't change. Expression is created by what the LIDS reveal or conceal. Eyes can shift position (thinking, curious) but not shape.

Speaking = Body Rhythm

Without a mouth, speaking is expressed through BODY movement - breath-like expansion/contraction. The lids provide very subtle engagement cues.

Three Elements in Harmony

Top lid + Bottom lid + Eyes all work together. Each has a role but none dominates. The orb (body) provides grounding with squash/stretch.

Subtle is Better

Real Ferni uses control points of -10 to +55 on a 100-unit viewbox. That's SUBTLE. Massive lid coverage looks wrong - it's about nuanced curves.
