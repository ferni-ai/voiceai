The Unified Expression System

🎭 Three as One

Top Lid + Bottom Lid + Eyes work as a unified face. They don't move independently - they move in relationship to each other. When the body squashes, the lids close in, and the eyes shift together.

🔊 Speaking = Rhythm

Not mechanical bouncing. Real speech has rhythm: emphasis on stressed syllables, pauses between thoughts, rising intonation for questions. All three elements pulse together on "Hi," "Fer-ni," "help," "to-day?"

💤 Sleepy = Lids Close In

The eyes don't change shape - they stay white ellipses. The LIDS close in from both directions, revealing less of the eyes. Like heavy eyelids closing. No pupils!

🤖 WALL-E Principle

Characters without mouths convey emotion through whole-body language. The entire face participates in every expression. This is how WALL-E, EVE, and BB-8 feel so alive.

The Speaking Animation

Instead of mechanical up-down bouncing, the speaking animation follows a natural phrase: "Hi, I'm Ferni. How can I help you today?"

Emphasis points: "Hi" (greeting pulse), "Ferni" (identity emphasis), "help" (strong engagement), "today?" (questioning rise)

Pauses: Natural breath between "Hi," and "I'm" - includes a blink. Settling moment after "Ferni."

Rising intonation: On "today?" the whole face opens up - lids pull back, eyes widen, body stretches up slightly.
